Getting ILR does not entitle you to a refund of Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) you have already paid. The financial benefit of ILR is that you stop paying IHS going forward — saving £1,035 per person per year for as long as you remain in the UK. IHS refunds are available in other circumstances: when an application is refused, withdrawn, curtailed, or cancelled.

One of the most common misconceptions in the UK immigration process is that getting ILR triggers a partial refund of the IHS you paid for your visa. It does not. The IHS is treated as payment for NHS access during your visa period — and that access is used.

What ILR does do is stop the IHS payments permanently. See [benefits of ILR](/articles/benefits-of-ilr-uk) for everything that changes when settlement is granted.

---

## Why ILR Does Not Trigger an IHS Refund

The Immigration Health Surcharge is payment for NHS access during your visa period. When you are on a Skilled Worker or family visa, the IHS funds your NHS access for the duration of that leave.

When ILR is granted, you have used NHS access during your visa — that period is not refundable. GOV.UK's IHS refund guidance explicitly excludes ILR applicants from the refund policy. The unused months between your ILR grant date and your original visa expiry are also not refunded.

This is different from:

- **A visa refusal:** Where you paid IHS upfront for leave you never received
- **A curtailment:** Where your leave was ended early and you paid for time that was taken away
- **A cancellation:** Where the Home Office revoked your leave

In those scenarios, the IHS is refundable because you did not receive what you paid for. In the ILR scenario, you received full NHS access throughout your visa period — so no refund applies.

---

## The Real Financial Benefit of ILR for IHS

The benefit of ILR is not a one-time refund — it is a permanent saving.

Once ILR is granted, you stop paying the IHS entirely. You access the NHS as an ordinary UK resident for the rest of your life.

**Example — family of four:**

On a Skilled Worker visa, each person pays £1,035/year in IHS. A family of four pays £4,140/year.

From the date of ILR, that annual charge stops. Over 10 years post-ILR, the family saves £41,400.

That is the real IHS benefit of ILR — not a refund, but never paying again.

## When Can You Get an IHS Refund?

IHS refunds ARE available in specific circumstances:

**1. Your application is refused**
If you paid IHS for a visa application and the application was refused, you can claim a refund for the IHS paid. You did not receive the leave, so the charge is refunded.

**2. Your application is withdrawn**
If you paid IHS and then withdrew your application before a decision, the IHS is refundable.

**3. Your leave is curtailed**
If the Home Office curtails your leave early — for example, because your employer's sponsor licence was revoked — you may be entitled to a refund for the remaining paid IHS period.

**4. Your visa is cancelled**
If the Home Office cancels your visa, the remaining IHS period may be refundable.

In all of these scenarios, you received less than what you paid for. The refund covers the unused portion.

---

## How to Apply for an IHS Refund

When you are in an eligible refund scenario, apply through the Home Office online refund service.

You will need:

1. Your IHS payment reference number (in the email confirmation from your original visa application)
2. The decision letter or curtailment notice
3. Bank account details for the refund payment
4. Details of any dependants also claiming

Apply as soon as the eligible event occurs. Processing times vary — apply promptly.

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Expecting an IHS refund when ILR is granted**
This is the most common IHS misconception. ILR does not trigger a refund of previously paid IHS. The benefit is stopping future payments — not recovering past ones.

❌ **Not claiming when legitimately entitled**
If your application was refused or your leave was curtailed, you are entitled to a refund. Many people do not claim because they are not aware the refund exists. If one of the qualifying scenarios applies to you, apply for the refund.

❌ **Not including dependants in the refund claim**
Each person who paid IHS and is in an eligible scenario can claim their own refund. Include all dependants — each refund claim is separate.

❌ **Losing the IHS payment reference number**
You need the reference number from the original visa application email to claim a refund. Keep all IHS payment confirmations stored digitally.

❌ **Waiting too long to apply after a refusal**
If your application was refused and you are entitled to a refund, apply as soon as possible. Do not assume the refund will be issued automatically.

## Expert Tips

**1. Stop confusing "refund" with "saving."** The IHS question for ILR applicants is not "will I get money back?" — it is "when does the charge stop?" The answer to the second question is: immediately on ILR grant.

**2. Calculate your annual IHS saving at the ILR planning stage.** At £1,035/year per person, a family of four saves £4,140/year from ILR day one. This is a powerful financial argument for applying for ILR as soon as you are eligible — every month of delay costs real money.

**3. If your visa was curtailed due to employer sponsor revocation, check whether an IHS refund applies.** This is one of the eligible scenarios. You paid for leave that was cut short through no fault of your own. The refund may be available.

**4. After British citizenship, IHS never applies again.** The permanent end to IHS is one of the practical arguments for progressing from ILR to citizenship. See the [requirements for British citizenship](/british-citizenship-requirements-2026) for the full checklist.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do you get an IHS refund when ILR is granted?

No. GOV.UK's IHS refund guidance explicitly excludes ILR from the refund policy. The IHS you paid during your visa period was for NHS access you received — it is not refunded when ILR is granted. The financial benefit of ILR is that you stop paying IHS going forward.

### When can you get an IHS refund?

IHS refunds are available when your application is refused, when you withdraw your application, when your leave is curtailed, or when your visa is cancelled. In these cases, you paid for access you did not fully receive — the unused portion is refunded.

### How much IHS do you save by getting ILR?

At £1,035 per person per year, the saving begins immediately on the ILR grant date. For a family of four, that is £4,140 per year. Over 10 years of post-ILR residence, the family saves £41,400 compared to remaining on visas indefinitely.

### How do you apply for an IHS refund?

Through the Home Office online refund service. You need your IHS payment reference number, the decision letter or curtailment notice, and bank details. Apply as soon as the eligible event occurs.

### Is the IHS refunded if a visa application is refused?

Yes. If you paid IHS for a visa application and the application was refused, you can claim a refund for the IHS paid. You did not receive the leave, so the charge is refundable.
