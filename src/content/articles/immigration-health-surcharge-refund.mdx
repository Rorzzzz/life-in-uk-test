You may be entitled to a partial refund of the Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) when you are granted ILR. The refund covers full months of IHS you paid for periods after your ILR grant date. It is not automatic — you must apply. The refund is processed by UKVI.

The IHS is paid upfront when you apply for a visa. If ILR is granted before your visa period ends, you have already paid for NHS access you no longer need to fund separately. A refund recovers that overpayment.

## How the IHS Refund Works

When you apply for a UK visa, you pay the IHS upfront for the full duration of your leave. At £1,035 per year for a 3-year Skilled Worker visa, that is £3,105 per person.

If ILR is granted at 2 years and 3 months into that 3-year visa, you have paid 9 months of IHS that you no longer need. The refund covers those 9 months.

**The calculation:**

At £1,035 per year, each full month equals £86.25.

9 months × £86.25 = £776.25 per person.

For a family of four, that refund is £3,105.

The month in which ILR was granted is not usually included — only full months after the grant date.

---

## Who Is Entitled to a Refund?

You are entitled to a refund if:

1. You were granted ILR before the end of your paid visa period
2. You personally paid IHS as part of your UK visa application
3. There are full months remaining in your paid IHS period after the ILR grant date

This applies to the main applicant and each dependent who paid IHS and was also granted ILR.

**You are not entitled to a refund if:**

- Your ILR was granted on the last day of your paid visa period
- Your employer paid the IHS on your behalf — the refund may belong to them, not you
- Your visa route was exempt from IHS

## How to Apply for an IHS Refund

The refund application is made through the Home Office's online refund service.

You will need:

1. Your IHS payment reference number (from your original visa application email confirmation)
2. Your ILR grant date and reference number
3. Your bank account details for the payment
4. Details of each dependent also claiming a refund

Apply as soon as your ILR is confirmed. [VERIFY: confirm current IHS refund processing time at GOV.UK before publishing]

## How Much Could You Receive?

At £1,035 per year, each full unused month is worth £86.25.

**Example — family of four:**

You applied for a 3-year Skilled Worker visa. IHS paid: £3,105 per person × 4 people = £12,420.

ILR is granted at 2 years and 4 months. That leaves 8 full months of IHS remaining per person.

Refund per person: 8 × £86.25 = £690.
Total refund for family of four: £690 × 4 = £2,760.

Apply for it.

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Not applying for the refund at all**
Many ILR holders do not know the refund exists or assume the amount is too small to pursue. For a family with several months of IHS remaining, the total can be thousands of pounds. Always apply.

❌ **Waiting too long to apply**
Apply as soon as ILR is granted. [VERIFY: confirm whether a deadline applies for IHS refund claims at GOV.UK]

❌ **Expecting the visa application fee to be refunded**
Only the IHS is refundable. The visa application fee is not. These are separate charges — the visa fee is non-refundable in all circumstances.

❌ **Not claiming for dependent children**
Each dependent child who paid IHS and was granted ILR is entitled to their own refund. Include all dependants in the application — the total can be significant.

❌ **Claiming a refund when IHS was employer-paid**
Some employers pay the IHS on behalf of their sponsored employees. If your employer paid your IHS, any refund may belong to them. Check your employment contract before applying.

## Expert Tips

**1. Apply for the refund the same week ILR is granted.** There is no financial reason to delay. The refund process starts from your application date.

**2. Calculate your expected refund before you apply.** Count the full months between your ILR grant date and your visa end date. Multiply by £86.25. If the refund you receive differs from your calculation, query it with UKVI.

**3. Find your IHS reference number before you need it.** It is in the email confirmation you received when you paid the IHS during your visa application. Locate it now and keep it with your ILR documents.

**4. If your employer paid the IHS, ask HR before claiming.** Some employers expect the refund to be returned to them. Others do not. Know which situation applies to you.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can you get a refund on the Immigration Health Surcharge?

Yes, a partial refund is available if you are granted ILR before your visa period ends. The refund covers full months of IHS paid for periods after your ILR grant date. It is not automatic — you must apply through the Home Office online refund service.

### How much of the IHS can you get back?

You can reclaim £86.25 for each full month of IHS paid after your ILR grant date. At £1,035 per year, that is the monthly value. A family of four with 8 unused months would receive £2,760.

### Is the IHS refund automatic when ILR is granted?

No. You must apply for the refund. It is not issued automatically when ILR is granted. Apply through the Home Office online service as soon as ILR is confirmed.

### Can dependants also get an IHS refund?

Yes. Each dependent who paid IHS and was granted ILR can claim their own refund. Include all dependants in your refund application — each one claims the unused months for their own IHS payment.

### Is the visa application fee refunded when ILR is granted?

No. Only the Immigration Health Surcharge is refundable. The visa application fee is non-refundable regardless of when ILR is granted or what happens during the visa period.
