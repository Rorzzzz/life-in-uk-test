From 8 January 2026, most ILR applicants must demonstrate B2 level English — not B1. This is a significant change. Many people applying now are still expecting the old B1 requirement. If you apply without B2 evidence and do not qualify for an exemption, your application will be refused.

Check your route carefully. The [full ILR requirements for 2026](/articles/british-citizenship-requirements-2026) include this English change as one of the biggest updates in years.

## What Changed on 8 January 2026

The Home Office raised the English language bar for ILR from B1 to B2 on 8 January 2026. This applies to most settlement routes.

B2 is the fourth level on the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR). It is one step above B1. The government's stated aim is to ensure settlers can participate more fully in UK society.

If you were already planning your ILR application expecting B1, you now need to reassess your English evidence.

---

## Which Visa Routes Are Affected

The B2 requirement applies to most settlement routes as of January 2026. The main routes affected include:

- Skilled Worker (ILR at 5 years)
- Family visas (spouse/partner route)
- Global Talent
- Innovator Founder
- Scale-up Worker

**Skilled Worker exception:** Skilled Worker applicants may use their employer's sponsorship letter as evidence of English ability in some circumstances. Check the current Home Office guidance for your specific situation — do not assume this applies to you without verifying.

Routes that remain at B1 or have their own English provisions should be checked individually on GOV.UK.

## Which English Tests Are Accepted for ILR in 2026

The Home Office accepts specific tests from approved Secure English Language Test (SELT) providers. For B2 ILR, the accepted options include:

**IELTS Life Skills B2**
Offered by British Council and IDP. Speaking and listening only. Results typically within 7 days.

**IELTS Academic or IELTS General Training**
A score demonstrating B2 level in speaking and listening (approximately 5.5 to 6.0 or above in those components). This is now accepted — previously these tests were not accepted for B1 ILR.

**Trinity College London ISE II**
Integrated Skills in English at B2 level. In-person assessment.

**LANGUAGECERT International ESOL B2**
Online proctored option available, making it accessible if you live far from a test centre.

Always check GOV.UK's approved SELT provider list before booking. This list is updated and providers can be added or removed.

## Is B2 Much Harder Than B1?

B2 is the level where you can understand complex texts and hold detailed conversations on unfamiliar topics. It is above everyday functional English.

At B1, you can handle routine situations. At B2, you can:

- Follow extended speech and discussions on abstract and complex topics
- Read and understand English news articles and professional communications
- Express yourself clearly and spontaneously without obvious effort

For most people who have been working in professional environments in the UK for several years, B2 is not out of reach. But it does require more preparation than B1 — do not assume your current level is B2 without testing it first.

## Who Is Still Exempt From the English Requirement

The exemption categories remain the same. You may not need a B2 test if:

**Nationality:** You are a national of a majority English-speaking country. The GOV.UK list includes Australia, Canada, New Zealand, USA, Jamaica, Barbados, and others. Check the full list on GOV.UK.

**Degree taught in English:** Your degree (Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD) was taught and assessed in English. You need documentary evidence — the degree certificate and possibly a letter from the institution confirming the language of instruction.

**Age:** You are 65 or over at the time of application.

**Disability or health:** A long-term condition prevents you from meeting the English requirement. You need a completed exemption form from your doctor plus original medical reports.

If you think you are exempt, verify on GOV.UK and gather the evidence. Do not assume — a wrong exemption claim leads to refusal.

## British Citizenship Is Still B1

This is important. Citizenship by naturalisation still requires B1 English — not B2.

So the pathway for most applicants is:

1. Pass B2 English test to qualify for ILR
2. Wait 12 months after being granted ILR
3. Apply for citizenship — B1 is sufficient

Your B2 certificate will clearly satisfy the B1 citizenship requirement. You do not need to take a separate lower-level test for citizenship.

## How Much Does a B2 English Test Cost

B2 English tests cost approximately £180 to £250 depending on the provider, test type, and location. IELTS Life Skills B2 and IELTS Academic/General Training prices vary by centre. LANGUAGECERT online options may differ. Check the provider's website for exact fees when booking.

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Applying with a B1 certificate after 8 January 2026**
If your application is made after 8 January 2026 and you submit only B1 English evidence without a valid exemption, it will be refused. The change is not transitional — it applies to all new applications made after that date.

❌ **Assuming your old B1 test result is sufficient**
A B1 certificate you obtained for a previous visa extension is not sufficient for ILR under the new rules. You need to demonstrate B2 for ILR now.

❌ **Confusing the ILR and citizenship requirements**
Citizenship is still B1. ILR is now B2. These are different applications with different standards. Do not mix them up when planning your preparation.

❌ **Not checking the approved SELT provider list**
Only tests from Home Office approved providers count. Taking a general English qualification from an unapproved provider wastes money and does not satisfy the requirement.

❌ **Leaving test booking too late**
B2 test slots are limited in many areas. Waiting times of 4-8 weeks are common in busy cities. Book as soon as you know your ILR application timeline.

❌ **Assuming Skilled Worker employer letter means no English test needed**
The employer sponsorship letter provision for Skilled Workers has specific conditions. Check the current rules for your situation — do not assume it applies automatically.

## Expert Tips

1. **Test your current level before booking.** Take a free online CEFR placement test to get a rough idea of whether you are at B2 already. If you score clearly at B2 or above, booking the test is lower risk. If you are borderline, take preparation time seriously.

2. **IELTS General Training now counts.** If you have recently taken or are planning IELTS General Training for another purpose, check if your scores meet the B2 threshold for speaking and listening. This could save you taking a separate test.

3. **Online options exist for B2.** LANGUAGECERT offers a proctored online B2 test you can take at home. This avoids travel to a test centre. Useful if you live in an area with limited test centre availability.

4. **Book B2 early in your ILR timeline.** ILR applications require a lot of evidence. Your English certificate is one document — but without it, the application is incomplete. Sort your B2 test several months before your planned application date.

5. **Keep your certificate permanently.** Your B2 certificate satisfies both the ILR English requirement and the lower B1 citizenship standard. One certificate covers both future milestones. Store it safely — losing it means retesting.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What English level do you need for ILR in 2026?

From 8 January 2026, most ILR applicants need B2 level English (CEFR). This replaced the previous B1 requirement. B2 is one level above B1 on the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages.

### Has the ILR English requirement changed?

Yes. The ILR English requirement changed from B1 to B2 on 8 January 2026. Applications submitted before that date under the old B1 rules were assessed under the old standard. All new ILR applications since that date require B2.

### Which English tests are accepted for ILR in 2026?

The accepted tests include IELTS Life Skills B2, IELTS Academic or General Training at B2 level scores, Trinity ISE II, and LANGUAGECERT International ESOL B2. All must be from Home Office approved SELT providers. Check GOV.UK for the current full list.

### How much does a B2 English test cost?

B2 English tests cost approximately £180 to £250 depending on the provider and location. IELTS Life Skills B2 prices vary by test centre. LANGUAGECERT online tests may differ. Check provider websites for exact current fees.

### Is B2 harder than B1?

Yes. B2 requires you to understand complex discussions, read professional-level English, and express yourself on a wide range of topics without obvious difficulty. B1 covers everyday functional communication. For most people who work in English in the UK, B2 is achievable with focused preparation — but it does require effort if you are at the lower end of B1.

### Does the B2 requirement affect citizenship?

No. British citizenship by naturalisation still requires B1 English. The B2 change only affects ILR. If you obtain ILR with a B2 certificate, that certificate also satisfies the B1 citizenship requirement — you do not need to take a separate lower test.

### Can Skilled Workers avoid the B2 test?

Some Skilled Worker applicants may use their employer's sponsorship letter as English evidence for ILR in certain circumstances. Check the current Home Office guidance for your specific route. Do not assume this applies without verifying.
