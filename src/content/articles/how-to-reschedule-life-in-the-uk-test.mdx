**Need to cancel or reschedule your Life in the UK test? Both work the same way.** Cancel your existing booking through your account at gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test, then book a new appointment if you need one. There is no direct "move" or "change date" button — cancelling and rebooking is the official process. You get a full refund only if you cancel at least **3 days (72 hours)** before your test. Cancel later than that and your £50 fee is lost.

⚠️ **Only use gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test.** A number of non-government sites rank in search results, look very similar to the official portal, and are not run by the Home Office or PSI Services. See the warning section below before you click any link from a search engine.

## ⚠️ Beware of Copycat Booking Websites

If you search "Life in the UK test reschedule" or "Life in the UK test booking," you will see results for sites that are **not** the official service. Some of these copy GOV.UK branding, Crown copyright notices, and even the wording of the real booking rules closely enough that they are easy to mistake for the genuine portal — even though they operate from non-government `.co.uk` domains rather than gov.uk.

**The only official booking and management portal is gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test.** It is the sole channel run on behalf of the Home Office, and it is where your booking, your payment, and your test centre confirmation actually need to be held. A third-party site cannot manage your real booking for you — at best you risk paying twice (once to them, once to the real system); at worst you risk handing over personal and payment details to a site with no accountability to the Home Office.

Before entering any details, check the address bar reads **gov.uk** — not a `.co.uk` domain with "test," "booking," or "lituk" in the name, however official it looks. If you are ever unsure whether a site is genuine, navigate to it directly by typing gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test yourself rather than clicking a search result or ad. If you believe you've paid a site that isn't the official service, contact your bank to query the transaction and report it to [Action Fraud](https://www.actionfraud.police.uk/).

## How to Reschedule — Step by Step

There is no single "reschedule" button. You change your test date in two steps: cancel the old booking, then create a new one.

**Step 1 — Cancel your existing booking:**

1. Go to [gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test](https://www.gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test) and sign in to your account
2. Select **"Confirmed tests"**
3. Select **"Cancel tests"** next to the booking you want to change
4. Confirm the cancellation

**Step 2 — Book your new date:**

5. From the same account, start a new booking
6. Choose your preferred test centre, date and time from the available slots
7. Pay the fee for the new appointment (£50 standard, £75 evenings/weekends, £80 within 24 hours)
8. Save your new confirmation email and booking reference

Do this in one sitting if you can. Cancelling first and waiting to rebook risks the slot you wanted being taken by someone else.

## The Refund Rule: 3 Days (72 Hours)

GOV.UK states plainly: **"You'll get a refund if you cancel your test at least 3 days (72 hours) before you're due to take the test."**

This is a straightforward 72-hour countdown from your appointment time — not a "working days" calculation. Weekends and bank holidays are not treated specially; what matters is whether you cancel 72 hours or more before your slot.

**Example:** Your test is booked for 2pm on a Friday. To get a refund, you must cancel by 2pm on the preceding Tuesday. Cancelling at any point after that — Tuesday afternoon, Wednesday, Thursday, or on the day — forfeits your £50.

If you cancel within the 72-hour window, or simply do not turn up, no refund is issued and you must pay again in full to book a new slot.

## What If the Test Centre Cancels Your Test?

Test centres occasionally cancel sittings due to technical failures, staffing issues, or emergency closures. If this happens to you:

- You will be notified, usually by email
- You are entitled to a full refund regardless of how close to your test date this happens
- You can rebook at another centre or date at no extra charge
- The refund should be automatic — contact the official helpline if it does not appear within a reasonable time

This is the one scenario where the refund is unconditional and not time-dependent, because the cancellation is not your doing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I cancel the Life in the UK Test and get a refund?

Yes. Sign in to your account at gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test, select "Confirmed tests," then "Cancel tests" next to your booking, and confirm. If you cancel at least 3 days (72 hours) before your appointment, your £50 fee is automatically refunded to the original payment card within a few working days. Cancel with less notice, or fail to attend, and no refund is given.

### Can I change my Life in the UK test date?

Yes. To change your Life in the UK test date, cancel your existing booking through your account at gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test, then book a new appointment for the date you want. There is no separate "change date" function — cancelling and rebooking is the official process. Cancel at least 3 days (72 hours) before your original appointment and you get a full refund; cancel later than that and the £50 fee is lost.

### Can I reschedule the Life in the UK test for free?

Yes — but "rescheduling" means cancelling and rebooking, and it is only free in the sense that you get your original £50 refunded. You must cancel at least 3 days (72 hours) before your appointment to qualify for that refund, then pay again for the new slot.

### Is there a "change date" or "reschedule" button on GOV.UK?

No. The official system does not have a direct reschedule function. You cancel your existing booking through "Confirmed tests" → "Cancel tests," then make a separate new booking. Some other guides describe a "Change appointment" button — this does not exist on the real GOV.UK system, so do not waste time looking for it.

### What happens if I cancel with less than 72 hours' notice?

You lose your £50 fee completely. There are no exceptions for illness, emergencies, or travel disruption under the standard policy. You will need to pay in full again for any new booking.

### Is "lituktestbooking.co.uk" or similar a legitimate site?

No third-party site can officially book or manage your Life in the UK test — the only channel run on behalf of the Home Office is gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test. Sites with similar names and similar branding are not part of the government service, however convincing they look. Always check that you are on a gov.uk address before entering any personal or payment details — and if in doubt, type the address in yourself rather than clicking a search result.

### How long does the refund take?

Refunds are returned to your original payment card. Processing times can vary — check your confirmation email or contact the official helpline (support@lituk.psionline.com / 0800 015 4245) if your refund has not arrived after a reasonable period. If you need to escalate to a formal complaint, this must normally be raised within **3 months of your test date**, and PSI aims to respond within **10 working days**.

### Is this different from a refund on my visa or citizenship application?

Yes. The £50 fee covered on this page is just for the Life in the UK test itself. If you mean a refund on a wider visa, settlement (ILR) or citizenship application fee, the rules are different: you can get a full refund if you cancel before submitting your biometrics or uploading your documents, but fees are normally non-refundable once your biometrics (fingerprints and photo) have been taken. To withdraw an application, use the cancel option on your GOV.UK account for that application — it is a separate process from cancelling your test booking.

### What happens if I just don't show up?

The outcome is the same as cancelling inside the 72-hour window: no refund, and your slot is recorded as a no-show. You can book a new appointment immediately afterwards — there is no penalty beyond the lost £50 and no block on rebooking.

### What if the test centre cancels my test?

You receive a full refund automatically and can rebook at another centre or date without paying again. This is the one scenario where the refund is unconditional regardless of timing — see above.

### Can someone else use my booking if I cannot attend?

No. Bookings are personal and cannot be transferred. If you cannot attend and someone else needs to take the test, they must create their own account and make their own booking.

### Can I move my test to a new date without it costing extra?

Yes — as long as you cancel at least 72 hours before your original appointment. You get the original £50 back in full, then use that refund to cover the new booking. Effectively, the only thing that changes is your date; the cost stays the same. Move inside the 72-hour window and you lose the original fee on top of paying for the new slot.

### How long do I have to make a complaint, and how quickly will PSI reply?

If something goes wrong with how your test or refund was handled, raise it within **3 months of your test date** — that's the window GOV.UK gives for complaints. PSI, who run the test on the Home Office's behalf, aim to respond within **10 working days** of receiving it. This complaints route is separate from the automatic £50 refund — it's for when the refund or booking process itself has gone wrong.

### I rescheduled because I wasn't ready — what should I do with the extra time?

Don't just sit on the new date — use the gap to close the gaps in your knowledge. Work through [practice questions by chapter](/practice) to find your weak topics, then run [timed mock exams](/mock-test) under real test conditions. Only treat your new appointment as confirmed once you're consistently scoring 90%+ — if you're still below that with a week to go, it's worth checking whether you can push the date out further.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Searching for a "reschedule" or "change date" button that does not exist**
The official system only offers cancel-then-rebook. Do not waste time hunting for a move function — go straight to "Confirmed tests" → "Cancel tests," then start a fresh booking.

❌ **Booking through a site that is not gov.uk**
Copycat sites rank highly in search results and copy official branding closely. Always check the address bar reads gov.uk before entering any personal or payment details.

❌ **Miscounting the 72-hour window**
This is a straight 72-hour countdown to your appointment time, not a "working days" calculation. Cancelling on a Wednesday morning for a Friday afternoon test may already be inside the 72-hour window — check the exact time, not just the date.

❌ **Cancelling before checking new slot availability**
Once you cancel, your old slot is gone. If your preferred test centre has limited availability, check that a suitable new slot exists before you cancel — or be ready to book immediately afterwards.

❌ **Assuming illness or emergencies are an exception**
There is no special exception for being unwell or having an emergency. If you cancel inside the 72-hour window for any reason, the £50 is forfeited. Build a buffer into your original booking date so you have room to reschedule for free if something comes up.

## Expert Tips

1. **Check new-slot availability before you cancel.** Search your preferred test centre and a few nearby alternatives for open dates first. Only cancel once you know a suitable replacement slot exists — this avoids being left without a booking.

2. **Build a buffer into your first booking.** If you book 4–6 weeks ahead rather than for the next available date, you give yourself room to cancel and rebook for free if your circumstances change.

3. **Use the extra time to actually improve your readiness.** If you are rescheduling because you are not confident, spend the extra days on [chapter practice](/practice) and [timed mock exams](/mock-test). Aim for 90%+ before you commit to your new date — that buffer matters more than the date itself.

4. **Save every confirmation email.** Keep both your cancellation confirmation and your new booking confirmation until after you have sat (and passed) your test. You may need the booking reference for ILR or citizenship applications later.
