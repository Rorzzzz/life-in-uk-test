Renewing a Skilled Worker visa is a standard part of the 5-year journey to ILR. Most people renew once — from a 3-year initial visa to a further 2 or 3 years — before reaching the ILR qualifying period.

When you are close to 5 years and eligible for ILR, consider whether to renew or apply for ILR directly. Use the [ILR eligibility calculator](/ilr-eligibility-calculator) to check your qualifying date.

## How to Renew a Skilled Worker Visa

Renewing is officially called applying for "further leave to remain" in the Skilled Worker category.

**Steps:**

1. Confirm your employer still holds a valid sponsor licence
2. Ask your employer to issue a new Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) for the renewal period
3. Gather supporting documents — payslips, passport, current BRP or eVisa details
4. Apply online at GOV.UK using the further leave to remain application
5. Pay the application fee and IHS upfront
6. Book and attend a UKVCAS appointment to submit biometrics if required

You apply while in the UK, using the online visa application system. You do not need to leave the UK to renew.

---

## When to Apply

Apply before your current visa expires. You can apply up to 3 months before your visa expiry date.

Do not leave the application to the last week. Processing times vary and delays are possible. If your visa expires while your renewal application is being processed — and you applied in time — Section 3C leave extends your right to remain and work.

If your visa expires before you apply, you are out of status. Do not let this happen.

---

## Salary Requirements for Renewal

To renew a Skilled Worker visa, you must meet the current salary threshold at the time of application.

**From April 2024:**

- General minimum salary: £38,700 per year (up from £26,200)
- Or the "going rate" for your specific occupation code — whichever is higher

Check GOV.UK for the current position on transitional salary protections — workers who were already sponsored before April 2024 may have had different thresholds apply at that time, but those protections are typically time-limited.

If your current salary is below the new threshold, you may need to negotiate a pay rise with your employer before renewing. If your employer will not meet the threshold, this is a significant problem — you cannot renew without meeting it.

---

## Certificate of Sponsorship for Renewal

Your employer issues a Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) to support your renewal. This confirms:

- Your employer is still sponsoring you
- Your role and salary details
- The period of the new sponsorship

If you are staying with the same employer in the same role, the process is straightforward. Your employer's HR or legal team typically handles the CoS.

If you are changing employer at the same time as renewing, your new employer issues the CoS. The new employer must be a licensed sponsor.

## What Happens to Your ILR Qualifying Period?

Your 5-year ILR qualifying period accumulates continuously through visa renewals.

If your first Skilled Worker visa was granted in June 2021 and you renew in June 2024 for another 2 years, your qualifying date is still June 2026 — 5 years from the original grant.

You do not restart the qualifying clock when you renew.

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Applying after the visa expiry date**
Once your visa expires, you are out of status. Apply for renewal before expiry — up to 3 months early. Do not wait until the last few days.

❌ **Not checking the salary threshold before applying**
If your salary has not increased in line with the April 2024 threshold changes, your renewal may be refused. Check the current threshold for your occupation code before submitting.

❌ **Assuming your employer's sponsor licence is still valid**
Sponsor licences can be revoked, suspended, or lapse. Before applying for renewal, confirm your employer's licence is still active on the GOV.UK register of licensed sponsors.

❌ **Not including the IHS in your budget**
The IHS is paid upfront with every renewal. At £1,035 per year, a 2-year renewal costs £2,070 in IHS per person, plus the visa application fee. Budget for this well in advance.

❌ **Renewing when you could apply for ILR instead**
If you are within 28 days of your 5-year qualifying date, apply for ILR rather than renewing the visa. ILR means no more renewals, no more IHS, and no more visa fees.

## Expert Tips

**1. Check the sponsor register before applying.** The GOV.UK register of licensed sponsors is public. Search for your employer's name to confirm their licence is active. A revoked licence means your renewal is invalid — and you face a 60-day curtailment period.

**2. Calculate whether to renew or apply for ILR.** If your qualifying date is within 6-12 months, consider whether a short renewal makes sense. Compare the renewal cost (application fee plus IHS) against applying for ILR directly. ILR stops the IHS permanently.

**3. The salary threshold applies at the time of the renewal application.** Make sure your current salary meets the threshold before you submit. A payslip from the most recent month is typically required.

**4. If you are close to the 5-year qualifying date, use the 28-day window.** You can apply for ILR up to 28 days before your qualifying date. See the [ILR 28-day rule guide](/ilr-28-day-rule-uk) for how to time this correctly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do you renew a Skilled Worker visa in the UK?

You apply for further leave to remain in the Skilled Worker category before your current visa expires. You need a Certificate of Sponsorship from your employer, must meet the current salary threshold, and apply online at GOV.UK. You do not need to leave the UK to renew.

### How early can you apply to renew a Skilled Worker visa?

You can apply up to 3 months before your current visa expiry date. Apply as early as the 3-month window allows — do not leave it to the final days.

### Does renewing a Skilled Worker visa reset the ILR qualifying period?

No. Your 5-year ILR qualifying period runs continuously from when your first Skilled Worker visa was granted. Renewing does not reset the clock. Your qualifying date remains 5 years from your original grant.

### What salary do you need to renew a Skilled Worker visa in 2026?

The general minimum salary threshold is £38,700 per year (from April 2024), or the going rate for your occupation code — whichever is higher. Always check the current immigration salary list on GOV.UK before applying, as thresholds are reviewed annually.

### What happens if my Skilled Worker visa expires before my renewal is decided?

If you applied before expiry, Section 3C leave automatically extends your existing leave and its conditions while the application is processed. You can continue working for your sponsor. Do not travel internationally while on Section 3C leave.
