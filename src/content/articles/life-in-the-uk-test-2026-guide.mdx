The Life in the UK test is a 24-question multiple-choice test required for anyone applying for Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) or British citizenship. It costs £50, takes 45 minutes, and requires 18 correct answers out of 24 to pass. This guide covers everything you need to know in one place.

The Life in the UK test 2026 is unchanged from previous years. It has 24 multiple-choice questions drawn from the official handbook, costs £50 for a standard slot, and requires 75 percent to pass. You take it in person at an approved test centre. The pass certificate is valid for life. Most people need two to four weeks of daily study to prepare successfully.

## Who Needs to Take the Life in the UK Test?

You must pass the test to apply for:
- **Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)** — permanent settlement in the UK
- **British citizenship** — naturalisation as a British citizen

You are exempt if you are under 18, aged 65 or over, or have a long-term physical or mental condition that prevents you from sitting the test. See the full [exemptions guide](/articles/life-in-the-uk-test-exemptions) for details.

If you hold EU Settled Status and are applying for citizenship, the same test applies — see [EU citizens and the Life in the UK test](/articles/eu-citizens-life-in-the-uk-test). Skilled Worker visa holders applying for ILR also need to pass — see [Skilled Worker visa and the Life in the UK test](/articles/skilled-worker-visa-life-in-the-uk-test).

## What the Test Covers

All 24 questions come from the official handbook — *Life in the United Kingdom: A Guide for New Residents (3rd edition)*. There is no outside knowledge required.

The five chapters and approximate question weighting:

| Chapter | Topic | Questions per test |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Values and principles of the UK | 1–2 |
| 2 | What is the UK? | 1–2 |
| 3 | A long and illustrious history | 8–12 |
| 4 | A modern, thriving society | 4–6 |
| 5 | The UK government, the law and your role | 3–5 |

Chapter 3 is the hardest and generates the most questions. See the full [topics breakdown](/articles/life-in-the-uk-test-topics) for a chapter-by-chapter guide to what is covered.

## How to Prepare

Most people need 2–4 weeks of daily study to pass. The most effective approach:

1. **Take a mock test first** — establish your baseline score before opening the handbook
2. **Read the full handbook** — every question comes from it, nothing else
3. **Use practice questions daily** — active recall beats passive reading
4. **Focus on Chapter 3** — it generates the most questions and is the hardest
5. **Memorise key dates and names** — these cause the most failures
6. **Book only at 90%+** — a practice score of 18–19 means you will likely fail

## How Much Does It Cost?

The standard fee is £50. This applies to slots booked between 10am and 4pm, Monday to Friday, with more than 48 hours' notice.

- **Out-of-hours or short-notice slots** (evenings, weekends, or less than 48 hours' notice): **£75**
- **Super Priority** (within 24 hours): **£80**

See the full [cost breakdown](/articles/how-much-does-the-life-in-the-uk-test-cost) for when each fee applies.

## How to Book

1. Go to [gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test](https://www.gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test)
2. Create an account and enter your details
3. Find your nearest test centre and choose a slot
4. Pay the £50 fee by card
5. Receive your booking confirmation by email

See the full [step-by-step booking guide](/articles/how-to-book-the-life-in-the-uk-test) for what ID you need and what to do if you need to change your appointment. ## What to Bring on Test Day

- Valid photo ID — same document used when booking (passport, BRP, or EU/EEA national identity card)
- Booking confirmation (printed or on your phone)
- Arrive at least 15 minutes early

Driving licences are not accepted. See the full [ID requirements guide](/articles/what-id-do-you-need-for-the-life-in-the-uk-test) for the complete list of accepted documents.

## What Happens on Test Day

The test is taken at a computer in a quiet room at your test centre. You have 45 minutes for 24 questions — most people finish in under 30 minutes. You can skip questions and return to them. There is no penalty for wrong answers.

Your result appears on screen immediately when you submit. If you pass, you receive a pass notification letter on the day. See the full [test day guide](/articles/life-in-the-uk-test-what-to-expect-on-the-day) for the complete walkthrough.

## What Happens After You Pass

Your pass certificate is valid for life — it never expires. You use it when submitting your ILR or citizenship application. You do not need to retake the test.

See our guide on [Life in the UK test results](/articles/life-in-the-uk-test-results) for exactly what happens after you submit — what the screen shows, what the certificate says, and how to use it.

---

## What Happens If You Fail

You can retake the test immediately — no waiting period. Pay £50 and book a new slot. Failed attempts are not reported to the Home Office. See the full guide on [what happens if you fail the Life in the UK test](/articles/what-happens-if-you-fail-the-life-in-the-uk-test).

The [pass rate](/articles/life-in-the-uk-test-pass-rate) is approximately 67% on first attempt — one in three people needs a retake. Most pass on their second attempt after targeted revision.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Has the Life in the UK test changed for 2026?

The test format, cost, and content remain the same in 2026. The fee structure was updated in 2025 to add Super Priority (£80) as a separate tier. Check GOV.UK for any recent changes.

### Can I take the test online?

No. The test must be taken in person at an approved test centre. There is no online version.

### Is the test the same for ILR and citizenship?

Yes. The same 24-question test, the same pass mark, and the same certificate apply for both applications.

### How long is the pass certificate valid?

For life. It never expires. A certificate from 2015 is still valid for a 2026 citizenship application.

### What ID do I need?

A valid passport, biometric residence permit (BRP), or EU/EEA national identity card. Driving licences are not accepted. See the [ID guide](/articles/what-id-do-you-need-for-the-life-in-the-uk-test).
