No Recourse to Public Funds (NRPF) is a condition attached to most UK visas. It prevents you from claiming most means-tested welfare benefits. NRPF does not block all benefits — it blocks the specific list of "public funds" defined in the Immigration Rules. ILR removes NRPF entirely.

## What Are "Public Funds"?

The Immigration Rules define "public funds" as a specific list of benefits. NRPF only blocks those on this list.

**Benefits blocked by NRPF (public funds under the Immigration Rules):**

- Universal Credit
- Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance
- Income-related Employment and Support Allowance
- Income Support
- Housing Benefit
- Child Benefit
- Child Tax Credit
- Working Tax Credit
- Council Tax Reduction (discretionary)
- Free school meals (in some circumstances)
- Homelessness assistance from a local authority
- Carer's Allowance
- Pension Credit

**Benefits NOT blocked by NRPF:**

NRPF does not block benefits based on National Insurance contributions:

- Contributory Jobseeker's Allowance (if you have paid sufficient NI)
- Contributory Employment and Support Allowance
- State Pension (if you have qualifying NI contributions)
- Statutory Maternity Pay (paid by employers, not government)
- Statutory Sick Pay (paid by employers — see [statutory sick pay explained](/articles/what-is-statutory-sick-pay-uk))
- NHS treatment (funded through the IHS you paid upfront)

## Who Has NRPF?

NRPF applies to most people on limited leave — a visa with conditions.

**Routes where NRPF typically applies:**

- Skilled Worker visa
- Health and Care Worker visa
- Family visa (spouse, civil partner, dependent)
- Student visa
- Graduate visa
- Most other temporary and limited leave routes

**Routes where NRPF does NOT apply:**

- ILR (Indefinite Leave to Remain)
- British citizens
- EU nationals with Settled Status under the EU Settlement Scheme
- Refugees and those with humanitarian protection (they have full access to public funds)

## Can You Get an Exception to NRPF?

Yes — in some cases. The Home Office can grant an exception to the NRPF condition if:

- You are destitute or at risk of destitution
- There are particularly compelling circumstances involving a child

An exception can be requested through the Home Office directly, or through a local authority if you approach them for support. The local authority has a duty of care to children even where a parent has NRPF.

This is not a straightforward process. Contact a local authority housing or social services team if you are in severe financial difficulty. You may also want to get advice from an immigration adviser.

---

## What Happens if You Claim a Public Fund While on NRPF?

Claiming a benefit on the public funds list while you have an NRPF condition is a serious issue.

It can result in:
- A decision to curtail your current leave
- Refusal of a future visa or ILR application on good character grounds
- In serious cases, removal from the UK

If you have accidentally claimed a benefit you were not entitled to, get legal advice. Do not assume the issue will not be discovered — Home Office and DWP data sharing means errors are identified.

## When Is NRPF Removed?

NRPF is removed when ILR is granted.

From the date of the ILR grant, you can claim most benefits — subject to the same eligibility tests as UK nationals. This includes the habitual residence test, which most long-term UK residents satisfy automatically.

Some benefits — notably Universal Credit — require you to demonstrate habitual residence in the Common Travel Area. For most ILR holders who have lived and worked in the UK for 5+ years, this is straightforward.

See the full [benefits of ILR](/articles/benefits-of-ilr-uk) for every right that is restored when settlement is granted.

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Thinking NRPF means you cannot use the NHS**
NRPF does not block NHS access. You paid the Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) upfront when you applied for your visa. That payment gives you NHS access as an ordinary resident during your visa period. NRPF and IHS are separate systems.

❌ **Claiming Child Benefit without checking your visa conditions**
Child Benefit is on the public funds list. If your visa has an NRPF condition, you cannot claim Child Benefit. Claiming it in error can affect future immigration applications.

❌ **Not knowing that NI-based benefits are accessible**
If you have paid sufficient National Insurance contributions, some contributory benefits are not blocked by NRPF. Many NRPF visa holders do not know this and assume all benefits are inaccessible.

❌ **Assuming the NRPF condition cannot be changed**
In cases of genuine hardship, an exception can be applied for. If you or your children are at risk of destitution, this option exists — it is not well publicised, but it is real.

❌ **Not checking that NRPF was actually removed after ILR was granted**
When ILR is granted, NRPF is removed automatically. But check your eVisa to confirm the NRPF condition no longer appears. If it does, contact UKVI — this should not happen but errors occur.

## Expert Tips

**1. Keep a record of every benefit claim you make or do not make.** If you ever need to demonstrate good character for ILR or citizenship, a clear record of not having claimed public funds while on NRPF is useful.

**2. Statutory Sick Pay is not a public fund.** It is paid by your employer. If you are off sick, you may be entitled to SSP even on an NRPF visa. Your employer pays it — not the government.

**3. If your circumstances change significantly — redundancy, relationship breakdown — get advice before assuming you have no options.** The NRPF exception process exists for situations of genuine hardship. Know it is there.

**4. Check your visa conditions on your eVisa.** Not all visa holders have NRPF — some routes do not include it. Check your specific conditions rather than assuming.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What does No Recourse to Public Funds mean?

NRPF is a condition on most UK visas that prevents you from claiming the specific welfare benefits listed as "public funds" in the Immigration Rules. It does not block all benefits — contributory benefits and NHS access are not affected.

### Which benefits can you not claim with NRPF?

You cannot claim Universal Credit, Housing Benefit, Child Benefit, Income Support, Working Tax Credit, Child Tax Credit, Carer's Allowance, Pension Credit, or council housing assistance. These are all on the public funds list.

### Does NRPF mean you cannot use the NHS?

No. NHS access is provided through the Immigration Health Surcharge, which you paid upfront with your visa application. NRPF does not affect this. You can see a GP, use hospital services, and access NHS treatment in the same way as a UK resident during your visa period.

### When is NRPF removed?

NRPF is removed when ILR (Indefinite Leave to Remain) is granted. From that point, you can claim most benefits, subject to the same eligibility tests as UK nationals. EU nationals with Settled Status also have NRPF removed.

### Can you get an exception to NRPF?

Yes, in cases of destitution or severe hardship — particularly where children are involved. Contact your local authority or the Home Office. This route is not widely known but is available as a genuine last resort.
