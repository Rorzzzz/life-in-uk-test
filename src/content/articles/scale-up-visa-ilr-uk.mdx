The 5 years does not need to be entirely on the Scale-Up visa — qualifying time on other routes may also count. After 6 months with your endorsing employer, you are free to work for any employer without needing a new sponsor.

## What Is the Scale-Up Visa?

The Scale-Up visa is for workers who have a job offer from an endorsing Scale-Up business. These are UK companies certified as high-growth scale-up businesses by an approved endorsing body.

The visa is initially granted for 2 years. It requires:

- A job offer from an endorsed Scale-Up employer
- A salary of at least £36,300 per year [VERIFY: confirm current Scale-Up minimum salary threshold at GOV.UK — may have changed with 2024 salary threshold updates]
- The role must be at a graduate-equivalent skill level

Unlike the Skilled Worker visa, the Scale-Up visa allows you to move freely between employers after an initial period.

## The 6-Month Rule: Freedom to Work

The most distinctive feature of the Scale-Up visa is the 6-month rule.

For the first 6 months of your Scale-Up visa, you must work for the endorsing employer who sponsored your entry. After 6 months, you can:

- Work for any UK employer in any qualifying role
- Change jobs without needing a new Certificate of Sponsorship
- Work in a role at a different salary (though tax and employment laws still apply)

This is significantly more flexible than the Skilled Worker visa, where changing employer requires a new sponsor and a new application.

## How Does the Scale-Up Visa Lead to ILR?

The Scale-Up visa is a qualifying route for ILR. You qualify after 5 years of continuous lawful residence.

**How the 5 years is counted:**

Time on the Scale-Up visa counts toward the 5-year qualifying period. If you switched to the Scale-Up visa from another qualifying route (such as Skilled Worker), time on the previous visa may also count — subject to the continuous residence rules.

[VERIFY: confirm at GOV.UK whether time on other qualifying routes before switching to Scale-Up counts toward the 5-year ILR qualifying period for this route]

**What happens after 5 years:**

You apply for ILR in the normal way — biometric appointment, Life in the UK test, English language, absence record. The Scale-Up route has no special ILR process. You use the standard ILR settlement application.

## Absence Rules on Scale-Up

The standard 180-day absence rule applies. You must not have spent more than 180 days outside the UK in any 12-month rolling period during your qualifying period.

For a 5-year qualifying period, there are 5 rolling windows to check. The Scale-Up route does not have more lenient absence rules than other ILR routes.

## Life in the UK Test on Scale-Up

The Life in the UK test is required for ILR on the Scale-Up route unless you are exempt.

Standard exemptions apply:
- Under 18
- Aged 65 or over
- Long-term physical or mental condition preventing you from taking the test

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Thinking 2 years on Scale-Up alone leads to ILR**
The Scale-Up visa is initially granted for 2 years. ILR requires 5 years of continuous lawful residence. You need to accumulate 5 qualifying years, not just complete the initial visa period.

❌ **Assuming the 6-month employer freedom removes all restrictions**
After 6 months, you can work for any employer. But you still must not overstay the Scale-Up visa period. If your 2-year visa expires, you must extend it before the qualifying period reaches 5 years.

❌ **Not tracking absences**
The 180-day rule applies on the Scale-Up route exactly as it does elsewhere. Frequent travel during the 5-year qualifying period can block ILR even if you have been on qualifying leave for the full time.

❌ **Confusing Scale-Up with Graduate visa**
Both visas offer work flexibility after entry. But the Graduate visa does not lead to ILR — time on Graduate does not count toward the qualifying period. Scale-Up does lead to ILR. They are different routes with different outcomes.

❌ **Not extending the visa before it expires**
The Scale-Up visa is granted for 2 years. If you want to stay beyond that and continue accumulating qualifying time for ILR, you need to extend it or switch to another qualifying route. Your continuous residence must remain lawful throughout.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does it take to get ILR on a Scale-Up visa?

ILR requires 5 years of continuous lawful residence. The Scale-Up visa is initially 2 years, so you need to renew or accumulate time across qualifying routes to reach 5 years. There is no shorter ILR qualifying period specific to the Scale-Up visa.

### Can I change jobs on a Scale-Up visa?

Yes — after 6 months working for the endorsing employer, you can work for any employer without needing a new sponsor or Certificate of Sponsorship. This flexibility is one of the main features of the Scale-Up route.

### Does the Scale-Up visa lead to permanent residence?

Yes. The Scale-Up route is a qualifying route for ILR. After 5 years of continuous lawful residence (which can include time on other qualifying routes), you can apply for ILR.

### What is the difference between Scale-Up and Graduate visa for ILR?

Scale-Up leads to ILR — time on the Scale-Up visa counts toward the 5-year qualifying period. The Graduate visa does not lead to ILR — time on Graduate does not count. To get ILR from the Graduate route, you must switch to a qualifying route such as Skilled Worker.

### What are the absence rules on the Scale-Up visa for ILR?

The standard 180-day rule applies — no more than 180 days outside the UK in any 12-month rolling period during the qualifying period. Check every rolling window with the [absence calculator](/absence-calculator) before applying.
