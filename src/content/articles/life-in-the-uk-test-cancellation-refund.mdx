## How to Cancel Your Life in the UK Test

You can only cancel online, through your account. There is no phone or email option.

1. Go to [gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test](https://www.gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test) and sign in
2. Select **"Confirmed tests"**
3. Select **"Cancel tests"** next to your booking
4. Confirm the cancellation

You will receive a confirmation email once it is processed. Keep this for your records — and to prove the time you cancelled, if there is ever a dispute about whether you met the deadline.

## When Do You Get a Refund?

The rule is a strict countdown, with no discretion built in.

**At least 3 days (72 hours) before your test — full refund.** GOV.UK states this directly: *"You'll get a refund if you cancel your test at least 3 days (72 hours) before you're due to take the test."* The fee returns to the card you paid with.

**Less than 72 hours before your test — no refund.** This applies even with a good reason — sudden illness, a family emergency, travel disruption. The policy does not vary by circumstance.

**Test centre cancels your test — full refund, always.** If the centre closes, has a technical fault, or cancels for any reason on its side, you are entitled to a full refund regardless of timing, and can rebook at no extra cost.

---

## How to Count the 72 Hours

This is where most people miscalculate. The rule is **72 hours before your appointment time** — not "3 working days," and not simply "3 days" on the calendar without checking the clock.

**Example:** Your test is booked for 10am on a Friday. The 72-hour cutoff falls at 10am on the preceding Tuesday. Cancelling at 11am on Tuesday — even just an hour later — already falls inside the window and forfeits your refund.

Weekends and bank holidays are not given special treatment in this calculation; what matters is the straight 72-hour gap between the moment you cancel and the moment your test is scheduled to start. When in doubt, cancel earlier than you think you need to — there is no penalty for cancelling with more notice than required.

---

## "Rescheduling" Is Cancelling and Rebooking — Not a Separate Process

Some guides describe rescheduling as a distinct option that lets you "change your date without losing your booking." **This is not how the official system works.** There is no direct reschedule or move-date function on gov.uk. To change your date, you cancel your existing booking and create a brand new one — which means the same 72-hour refund rule applies, because you are, in effect, cancelling.

If you cancel with 72+ hours' notice, you get your £50 back and pay again for the new slot — netting out to no extra cost beyond the admin of rebooking. If you cancel with less notice, you lose the £50 and must pay in full for the new appointment. There is no version of "changing your date" that avoids this rule.

## What If the Test Centre Cancels?

Test centres occasionally cancel sittings due to technical failures, staffing issues, or emergency closures. If this happens to you:

- You will be notified, usually by email
- You are entitled to a full refund
- You can rebook at another centre or date at no extra charge
- The refund should be automatic — contact the official helpline if it does not appear within a reasonable time

This is the one scenario where the refund is unconditional and not time-dependent, because the cancellation is not your doing.

---

## Is There Any Exception for Emergencies?

No. GOV.UK does not publish — and does not operate — a discretionary exceptional-circumstances policy for late cancellations. Hospitalisation, bereavement, and other serious situations are not treated differently from a simple change of plan. The 72-hour cutoff is enforced automatically by the booking system with no manual review step.

The only real protection is timing: cancel while you are still outside the 72-hour window, even if you are not yet certain you will need to.

## After You Cancel — Rebooking

Once your cancellation is confirmed, you can book a new test immediately. You will need to:

- Pay the fee again (£50 standard, £75 evening/weekend, £80 within 24 hours)
- Choose a test centre, date and time from available slots
- Save your new confirmation email and booking reference

There is no waiting period and no limit on how many times you can book and sit the test. If you are still preparing, use our [free mock tests](/mock-test) and [practice questions by chapter](/practice) to get ready before you commit to a new date.

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Trying to cancel by phone or email.** Neither is accepted. Only an online cancellation through your gov.uk account counts — anything else leaves your booking active and your fee at risk.

❌ **Counting "3 days" on the calendar instead of 72 hours on the clock.** The cutoff is based on the exact time of your appointment. Cancelling on "the right day" but after the cutoff hour still forfeits your refund.

❌ **Assuming illness changes the outcome.** It does not. There is no exception process. If you are inside the 72-hour window, the fee is gone whether or not you attend.

❌ **Booking through a site that is not gov.uk.** Several non-government sites mimic the look of the official booking pages closely. Only gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test can actually manage your real booking — always verify the address bar before entering any details.

❌ **Waiting to "see how things go" before cancelling.** Every hour spent deciding while still eligible is an hour you cannot get back if the window closes. If there is real doubt, cancel now — you lose nothing by cancelling early.

## Expert Tips

1. **Cancel the moment you know, not the moment you're sure.** If there's a real chance you can't attend, cancel while you're still outside the 72-hour window. You can always book a new slot — but you cannot undo a missed deadline.

2. **Save your cancellation confirmation with its timestamp.** If a dispute ever arises about whether you cancelled in time, the confirmation email is your evidence.

3. **Build a buffer into your original booking.** Booking 4-6 weeks ahead, rather than the earliest available slot, gives almost any sudden problem room to surface while you're still safely outside the 72-hour window.

4. **Check new-slot availability before you cancel.** Once your booking is gone, it's gone. If you plan to rebook immediately, make sure a suitable slot exists first — see our guide on [Life in the UK test booking wait times](/articles/life-in-the-uk-test-booking-wait-time).

## FAQs

### Can I cancel my Life in the UK test and get a refund?

Yes — if you cancel at least 3 days (72 hours) before your appointment. Cancel with less notice, or fail to attend, and no refund is given. The standard fee is £50 (weekday), £75 (evening/weekend) or £80 (within 24 hours).

### How do I cancel my Life in the UK test?

Sign in to your account at gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test, select "Confirmed tests," then "Cancel tests" next to your booking, and confirm. This is the only official channel — third-party sites cannot cancel your booking for you.

### What happens if I miss my Life in the UK test?

You lose your fee. There is no automatic refund for non-attendance, and the outcome is the same as cancelling inside the 72-hour window. You can rebook immediately, but you must pay in full again.

### What if the test centre cancels my test?

You receive a full refund automatically and can rebook at another centre or date without paying again. This is the one scenario where the refund is unconditional regardless of timing.

### How long does the refund take?

Refunds return to your original payment card. Allow a reasonable number of working days for it to appear, and contact the official helpline (support@lituk.psionline.com / 0800 015 4245) if it has not arrived after that.

### Can I cancel by email or phone?

No. Cancellations must be made online, through your account at gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test. Phone and email requests are not processed as cancellations.

### Is there an exception to the 72-hour rule for illness or emergencies?

No. GOV.UK does not operate a discretionary exceptions process for the standard refund policy. The 72-hour cutoff applies uniformly, regardless of the reason for the change.

### Can I rebook straight away after cancelling?

Yes. Once your cancellation is confirmed, you can immediately book a new test date. You will pay the full fee again for the new appointment.
