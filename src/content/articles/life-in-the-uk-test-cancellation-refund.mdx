## How to Cancel Your Life in the UK Test

You can only cancel your test online. There is no phone cancellation option.

Follow these steps:

1. Go to [gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test](https://www.gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test)
2. Log in to your booking account
3. Find your upcoming test booking
4. Select the option to cancel
5. Confirm the cancellation

You will receive a confirmation email once the cancellation is processed. Keep this for your records.

## When Do You Get a Refund?

The refund rule depends entirely on how much notice you give.

**3 or more working days before your test — full refund.** The fee is returned to the card you paid with. Allow a few days for it to appear on your statement.

**Less than 3 working days — no refund.** This applies even if you have a good reason, such as a sudden illness or work emergency. The policy is firm.

**Test centre cancels your test — full refund, always.** If the test centre closes, has a technical problem, or cancels for any reason, you are entitled to a full refund regardless of when it happens. You also have the right to rebook at no additional cost.

---

## How to Count 3 Working Days

This is where many people go wrong. Working days are Monday to Friday, not including weekends or public bank holidays.

Here is an example. Your test is on a Thursday. To qualify for a refund, you must cancel by the end of Monday (3 working days before: Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday — Thursday is the test day). Cancelling on Tuesday gives you only 2 working days' notice — no refund.

If your test is on a Monday, you must cancel by the previous Wednesday. The weekend does not count.

During periods with bank holidays — such as Easter or Christmas — add extra days to your count. A bank holiday is not a working day.

---

## Cancelling vs Rescheduling

These are two different things.

**Cancelling** means you remove your booking completely and (if eligible) receive a refund. You then need to rebook from scratch and pay the fee again.

**Rescheduling** means you change the date of your existing booking without cancelling it. You must give at least 3 days (72 hours) notice to reschedule without losing your fee. Log in to your GOV.UK booking account to move the date.

If rescheduling is an option for your situation, it is usually better than cancelling, because you avoid having to pay again.

For more detail on changing your booking, see our guide on [how to reschedule your Life in the UK test](/articles/how-to-reschedule-life-in-the-uk-test).

## What If the Test Centre Cancels?

Test centres occasionally cancel tests due to technical failures, staff shortages, or emergency closures. If this happens to you:

- You will be notified, usually by email
- You are entitled to a full refund
- You can also rebook at another centre at no extra charge
- The refund should be automatic, but contact the test centre if it does not appear within a few days

This situation is outside your control, so the refund policy is unconditional.

---

## Exceptional Circumstances

GOV.UK does not publish a formal exceptional circumstances policy for last-minute cancellations. If you face a serious emergency — hospitalisation, bereavement, or a similar situation — contact the test centre directly. Keep any evidence (a doctor's letter, for example). There is no guarantee of a discretionary refund, but it is worth asking.

## After You Cancel — Rebooking

Once you have cancelled, you can rebook immediately. You will need to:

- Pay the test fee again (£50 standard, £75 evening/weekend, £80 within 24 hours)
- Choose a new test centre and date
- Receive a new confirmation email

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Cancelling by phone.** Phone cancellations are not accepted. Your test will not be cancelled and you will not get a refund. Only online cancellations work.

❌ **Miscounting working days.** People include weekends or bank holidays in their 3-day count. This leads to cancelling too late and losing the refund.

❌ **Waiting to see if you feel better.** If you are ill and your test is in 2 days, do not wait. By the time you know you cannot attend, it may be too late to cancel with enough notice.

❌ **Assuming rescheduling and cancelling are the same.** They are not. Cancelling means you lose your booking and must pay again. Check the rescheduling option first.

## Expert Tips

1. **Cancel as soon as you know.** Even if you are unsure whether you will recover from an illness or resolve a conflict, cancel early to protect your refund. You can always rebook.

2. **Screenshot your cancellation confirmation.** Disputes do happen. A screenshot with a timestamp proves you cancelled in time.

3. **Check the calendar before you count.** Open a calendar and count working days manually, including any bank holidays. Do not rely on mental arithmetic.

4. **Consider rescheduling instead.** If you just need more study time, rescheduling may cost you nothing — whereas cancelling and rebooking means paying again.

5. **Use the absence calculator before rebooking.** If ILR is time-sensitive, check your [absence calculator](/absence-calculator) before picking a new test date.

## FAQs

### Can I cancel my Life in the UK test and get a refund?

Yes, if you cancel at least 3 working days before your test date. Cancel with less notice and no refund is given. The standard fee is £50 for weekday tests, £75 for evening or weekend tests, and £80 for tests within 24 hours.

### How do I cancel my Life in the UK test?

Log in to your booking account at gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test and find your upcoming booking. Select the option to cancel and confirm. You cannot cancel by phone — it must be done online.

### What happens if I miss my Life in the UK test?

If you miss your test without cancelling, you lose your fee. There is no automatic refund for non-attendance. You will need to rebook and pay again.

### What if the test centre cancels my test?

If the test centre cancels your booking, you receive a full refund automatically. You can also rebook at another centre without paying again.

### How long does the refund take?

Refunds are returned to the original payment card. Allow a few business days for the refund to appear on your statement.

### Can I cancel by email or phone?

No. Cancellations must be made online through your GOV.UK booking account. Phone and email cancellations are not accepted.

### What is a working day for the 3-day rule?

Working days are Monday to Friday, excluding weekends and UK public bank holidays. Count carefully, especially around Easter and Christmas.

### Can I rebook straight away after cancelling?

Yes. Once your cancellation is confirmed, you can immediately log in and book a new test date. You will pay the full fee again.
