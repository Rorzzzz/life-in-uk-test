## Why the Test Cannot Be Taken Online

The Life in the UK test is an official immigration requirement administered by the Home Office. It must be taken in person to verify your identity and prevent fraud. When you arrive at the test centre, you present your photo ID, check in with a member of staff, and complete the test on a computer at the centre.

The test is computer-based — you answer the 24 questions on a screen — but the computer is at the test centre, not at your home.

## How to Book the Real Test

1. Go to [gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test](https://www.gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test)
2. Create a GOV.UK account
3. Choose your nearest test centre and available date
4. Pay the £50 fee by card
5. Receive your confirmation email

See the full [booking guide](/articles/how-to-book-the-life-in-the-uk-test) for step-by-step instructions including what ID to bring.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is there an official online Life in the UK test?

No. The official test can only be taken at an approved test centre. The Home Office does not offer any online, remote or at-home version of the test.

### Can I take the Life in the UK test on my phone or laptop at home?

No. The test is taken on a computer at an approved test centre. You cannot take it on your own device.

### Are online practice tests the same as the real test?

No, but they cover the same material. Practice tests use questions based on the official handbook — the same source as the real test. Practising with them is the most effective way to prepare.

### What happens if I cannot travel to a test centre?

If you have a long-term physical or mental condition that prevents you from taking the test, you may qualify for an [exemption](/articles/life-in-the-uk-test-exemptions). Contact the Home Office to discuss your options.

### Can I take the test in Welsh or another language?

Yes — Welsh and Scottish Gaelic versions are available at certain test centres by prior arrangement. See the full guide on [taking the test in Welsh or Scottish Gaelic](/articles/life-in-the-uk-test-in-welsh-scottish-gaelic).

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Assuming online practice scores count.**
Completing online practice tests does not count as passing the real test. You must book and sit the official test at a test centre regardless of your practice scores.

**Booking through third-party sites.**
Only book through the official GOV.UK page. Third-party booking sites charge additional fees and are not endorsed by the Home Office.

**Confusing the booking process with the test itself.**
You book online, but you sit the test in person. These are two separate steps.
