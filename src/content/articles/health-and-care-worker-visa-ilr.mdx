Health and Care Worker visa holders can apply for ILR after 5 years of continuous lawful residence. The qualifying period, absence rules, and ILR application process are the same as the Skilled Worker route. The key difference is that the Health and Care Worker visa has lower salary thresholds and is reserved for healthcare roles.

## What Is the Health and Care Worker Visa?

The Health and Care Worker visa is a sub-route of the Skilled Worker visa. It is specifically for:

- NHS doctors, nurses, and allied health professionals
- Health and social care workers in eligible registered care settings
- Roles that appear on the eligible occupation list for this route

The main advantages over the standard Skilled Worker route:
- Lower salary thresholds
- No Immigration Health Surcharge (NHS workers are exempt — the IHS exemption applies to most Health and Care Worker visa holders employed by the NHS or registered care providers)
- Faster processing in some cases

## How the 5-Year Qualifying Period Works

The ILR qualifying period for the Health and Care Worker route is 5 years of continuous lawful residence.

The 5 years:
- Starts from the date your first Health and Care Worker (or previous qualifying) visa was granted
- Accumulates through visa renewals on the same route
- Must be in an eligible role throughout (role changes within the route must remain eligible)

If you switch from a different qualifying route (for example, an earlier Skilled Worker visa), that time may also count toward the 5-year period.

## Absence Rules on This Route

The standard 180-day absence rule applies exactly as it does on the Skilled Worker route.

You must not have spent more than 180 days outside the UK in any single 12-month rolling period during the qualifying period.

Healthcare workers who travel frequently for conferences, visits to family in their home country, or other reasons should track this carefully. The [absence calculator](/absence-calculator) checks every rolling window automatically.

## Life in the UK Test for Healthcare ILR

The Life in the UK test is required for ILR regardless of the route you are on. It cannot be waived based on your profession.

Standard exemptions apply — under 18, aged 65 or over, or certain long-term conditions. Most healthcare workers must pass the test.

Prepare with the [free practice questions](/practice). Healthcare workers in particular often find the history and culture chapters the most challenging — these are the sections most different from your professional knowledge.

## Dependant Rules — Important Changes

Dependant rights on the Health and Care Worker visa have been subject to restrictions.

From March 2024, care workers and home carers on this route are no longer permitted to bring dependants to the UK (spouse, civil partner, or children).

This restriction does not apply to healthcare professionals employed directly by the NHS or in NHS-funded roles.

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Assuming IHS is always free on this route**
The IHS exemption on the Health and Care Worker visa applies to workers employed by eligible NHS bodies and registered care providers. Not all employers in healthcare qualify. Check your specific employer before assuming the IHS is waived.

❌ **Thinking the healthcare context makes ILR easier**
The ILR application process for Health and Care Worker visa holders is the same as for standard Skilled Worker holders. There is no simplified or expedited ILR pathway based on your role in healthcare.

❌ **Ignoring absence records**
Healthcare workers are often asked to attend international conferences, training abroad, or travel home to see family. The 180-day rolling absence rule is the same as any other route. Every trip counts.

❌ **Not tracking role eligibility during the qualifying period**
If you change role during the 5-year period, the new role must still be an eligible Health and Care Worker occupation. Moving into a non-eligible management or administrative role while keeping the same employer can affect route eligibility.

❌ **Not knowing about the dependant restriction before renewing**
If your visa is due for renewal and the care worker dependant restriction applies to you, a renewal may not allow you to bring or keep dependants. Check before you renew.

## Expert Tips

**1. Confirm the IHS exemption applies to your specific employer before your visa is issued.** The exemption is tied to the type of employer, not just the job. NHS trust employees are typically exempt. Private care agencies may not be. Check in advance.

**2. Keep all payslips and role confirmation letters throughout the 5 years.** Your ILR application will require evidence of employment throughout the qualifying period. Payslips are the clearest evidence. Store them digitally.

**3. If you change from Health and Care Worker to a standard Skilled Worker role, your qualifying period continues.** Both are qualifying routes. The switch itself does not reset the clock — but your new role must meet the standard Skilled Worker salary thresholds.

**4. Start the Life in the UK test preparation early.** Use the [free practice questions](/practice) in the months before your qualifying date. The test is the same regardless of your profession — clinical knowledge does not help with history and government questions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long to get ILR on a Health and Care Worker visa?

5 years of continuous lawful residence. The qualifying period starts from when your first Health and Care Worker (or other qualifying) visa was granted. It accumulates through renewals.

### Is the Health and Care Worker visa a qualifying route for ILR?

Yes. The Health and Care Worker route leads directly to ILR after 5 years. It is a qualifying route under the Immigration Rules.

### Do Health and Care Worker visa holders need the Life in the UK test?

Yes — unless exempt. The same exemptions apply as for all other ILR routes: under 18, aged 65 or over, or certain long-term conditions. Healthcare professionals must pass the test.

### Is the Immigration Health Surcharge waived on the Health and Care Worker visa?

The IHS is exempt for workers employed by eligible NHS bodies and registered care providers. [VERIFY: confirm full list of qualifying employer types at GOV.UK] Not all healthcare employers qualify — check your specific employer type before assuming the exemption applies.

### What are the salary thresholds for the Health and Care Worker visa?
