## What Is B1 English Level?

B1 is the third level on the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR). The levels run from A1 (complete beginner) to C2 (mastery). B1 sits in the middle:

- **A1–A2**: Basic English — simple phrases, familiar situations
- **B1**: Intermediate — main points of clear speech, everyday situations
- **B2**: Upper intermediate — complex topics, more abstract language
- **C1–C2**: Advanced to mastery

At B1 level, you can:

- Understand the main points of conversations on familiar topics (work, travel, daily life)
- Handle most situations you encounter in the UK — shops, doctors, councils, employers
- Describe experiences, events and opinions in clear sentences
- Follow the main points of news broadcasts and straightforward texts

B1 is not advanced English. If you have been living and working in the UK for several years and communicating in English daily, you are almost certainly already at B1 or higher. The official test is simply the formal proof that you meet the requirement.

## Why Do You Need B1 English for ILR and Citizenship?

The English language requirement for ILR and British citizenship is set in the Immigration Rules. The Home Office requires applicants to demonstrate they can communicate in English at a level that enables them to participate fully in UK life and employment.

For ILR: the B1 requirement applies to speaking and listening. You do not need to demonstrate reading or writing skills for the ILR English language test.

For British citizenship: the same B1 requirement applies. If you already hold a B1 certificate from your ILR application, the same certificate may be used for your citizenship application — check the validity rules below.

The B1 requirement is separate from the [Life in the UK test](/articles/do-i-need-a-b1-english-test-for-ilr). Passing the knowledge test does not count as evidence of English language ability. You need both documents for a complete ILR application. See the full guide on [what to do after passing the Life in the UK test](/articles/what-to-do-after-passing-life-in-the-uk-test) for the full document checklist.

## What Does the Official B1 Test Involve?

The B1 speaking and listening test is conducted with a live examiner — it is not a multiple-choice computer test.

Here is what to expect:

**Trinity GESE Grade 5 and Trinity ISE I (in-person)**  
A spoken assessment lasting approximately 10–12 minutes. You will be asked to discuss a topic of your choice, respond to the examiner's questions, and complete a short interaction task. The examiner assesses fluency, vocabulary, grammar and pronunciation at B1 level.

**IELTS Life Skills B1 (in-person)**  
A speaking and listening test taken with another candidate in front of an examiner. The test has a discussion task and a question-and-answer section. It lasts approximately 22 minutes including the listening component.

**LANGUAGECERT (online from home)**  
A secure proctored online exam. You take the test at home using a webcam and microphone. The exam is monitored remotely. It covers speaking and listening at B1 level. This is the only UKVI-approved B1 test that can currently be taken online from home.

None of these tests requires you to write anything. All are speaking and listening assessments only.

## Which B1 Test Should You Choose?

**LANGUAGECERT** is the most convenient option for most people. You can take it from home without travelling to a test centre. It is fully accepted for UKVI purposes. If test centre availability is limited in your area, LANGUAGECERT is often the fastest option.

**Trinity GESE Grade 5** is widely available and well established. It is a shorter test than IELTS Life Skills and is often the most straightforward option for people who prefer face-to-face assessment.

**IELTS Life Skills B1** is run by the British Council and IDP. It is taken at British Council centres across the UK. If you have already used IELTS for other immigration purposes, the format may feel familiar — though the Life Skills test is different from standard IELTS Academic or General Training.

All three are equally accepted by the Home Office. The choice comes down to location, availability, and personal preference. Check current fees at each provider's website before booking — prices vary.

## Who Is Exempt From the B1 English Requirement?

You may not need to take a B1 test if you fall into one of the following categories:

**Skilled Worker visa holders**: Most Skilled Worker (formerly Tier 2) applicants demonstrated English at B1 level or above when they applied for their visa. This exemption carries through to ILR — you do not need to take the B1 test again if you already met the English language requirement at the visa stage.

**Majority English-speaking nationalities**: If you are a national of one of the following countries, you are exempt: Antigua and Barbuda, Australia, The Bahamas, Barbados, Belize, British overseas territories, Canada, Dominica, Grenada, Guyana, Jamaica, Malta, New Zealand, St Kitts and Nevis, St Lucia, St Vincent and the Grenadines, Trinidad and Tobago, USA. Ireland nationals are exempt for citizenship applications only.

**Degree taught in English**: If you hold a degree (Bachelor's, Master's or PhD) that was taught and assessed entirely in English, you may be exempt. Check GOV.UK for the current evidence requirements — you will need your degree certificate and may need a letter confirming the language of instruction.

**Age 65 or over**: Applicants aged 65 or above are exempt from the English language requirement.

**Long-term physical or mental condition**: If a condition prevents you from meeting the English requirement, you may qualify for an exemption with supporting medical evidence. A completed exemption form from a doctor and original medical reports are required.

Always verify your exemption status at GOV.UK before assuming you are exempt. An incorrect exemption claim can cause your application to be refused.

## How to Practise for Your B1 English Test

For most applicants who use English daily, the B1 test does not require intensive preparation. Here is a practical approach:

**Step 1: Check your level first.** [Try our free B1 English level check](/b1-check) — 15 questions covering vocabulary, grammar and reading comprehension. If you score well, you are likely ready to book.

**Step 2: Familiarise yourself with the test format.** Download the sample materials from your chosen provider's website. Each provider publishes example tasks so you know what to expect on the day.

**Step 3: Practise speaking in English.** The B1 test is a spoken assessment. If you are not regularly speaking English at work or socially, spend a few weeks having conversations in English — with colleagues, family members, or via language exchange apps.

**Step 4: Prepare for the specific tasks.** Trinity GESE requires you to talk about a topic of your choice. Choose a topic you know well (your job, a hobby, your neighbourhood) and practise talking about it for 2–3 minutes fluently.

**Step 5: Book early.** Test centre availability varies by location. Some centres have waiting times of 4–6 weeks. Book as soon as you know you need the test.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Taking the wrong IELTS test**
IELTS Academic and IELTS General Training are not accepted for ILR or citizenship English language purposes. You need specifically IELTS Life Skills B1. These are completely different tests. If you take the wrong one, it will not count and you will need to retake.

❌ **Assuming any English qualification counts**
Only the approved UKVI tests are accepted: Trinity GESE Grade 5, Trinity ISE I, IELTS Life Skills B1, and LANGUAGECERT. A GCSE in English, a workplace English qualification, or a general language certificate from another provider will not be accepted.

❌ **Leaving the test too late**
Test centre availability can be limited. Some locations have a 4–6 week wait for appointments. If you are approaching your ILR application window, book the B1 test immediately — do not wait until you have passed the Life in the UK test first.

❌ **Thinking an expired certificate cannot be used**
B1 certificates are generally valid for 2 years. However, for settlement (ILR) applications, an expired certificate may be used if it is on the current approved list and was previously accepted for another UK immigration application. For citizenship, an expired certificate may be used if it was accepted when settlement was granted. Check GOV.UK for your specific situation.

❌ **Not checking whether you are exempt before booking**
If you are a Skilled Worker visa holder, a national of a majority English-speaking country, or hold a degree taught in English, you may not need the test at all. Booking an unnecessary test wastes money and time.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does the B1 English test cost?

The cost is £150–£215 depending on the provider, test type, and location. LANGUAGECERT, Trinity, and IELTS Life Skills all have different fee structures. Check the provider's website for your nearest test centre's current fees.

### Can I take the B1 test online?

Yes — LANGUAGECERT offers a fully online proctored exam that can be taken from home. Trinity GESE and IELTS Life Skills are available in-person only at approved test centres.

### How long is my B1 certificate valid for?

Generally 2 years from the test date. Exceptions: for ILR applications, an expired certificate may be used if it was previously accepted for a UK immigration application and remains on the approved list. For citizenship, an expired certificate is accepted if it was accepted when settlement was originally granted. Check GOV.UK for your specific circumstances.

### Do I need the B1 test if I have a Skilled Worker visa?

Most Skilled Worker visa holders are exempt from the B1 test at ILR stage — they demonstrated English at B1 or above to obtain their visa. Check GOV.UK to confirm this applies to your specific visa and route.

### What happens if I fail the B1 test?

You can retake it. There is no limit on retakes and no mandatory waiting period between attempts. Each attempt costs the standard test fee. If you are struggling, request feedback from the test provider and address the specific areas where you lost marks.

### Is the B1 test the same as the Life in the UK test?

No. The B1 English test assesses your ability to speak and listen in English — it is conducted with a live examiner. The [Life in the UK test](/articles/do-i-need-a-b1-english-test-for-ilr) is a 24-question computer-based knowledge test about British history and society. They are two completely separate requirements, and you need both for ILR.

### Which nationalities are exempt from the B1 English test?

Nationals of: Antigua and Barbuda, Australia, The Bahamas, Barbados, Belize, British overseas territories, Canada, Dominica, Grenada, Guyana, Jamaica, Malta, New Zealand, St Kitts and Nevis, St Lucia, St Vincent and the Grenadines, Trinidad and Tobago, USA. Ireland nationals are exempt for citizenship only.
