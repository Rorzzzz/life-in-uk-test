You can apply for British citizenship **12 months after your ILR was granted**. Most people on standard work or family routes follow this rule. The exact date is on your eVisa or was on your Biometric Residence Permit (BRP).

You can submit your application up to **28 days before** that 12-month date. Applying earlier than 28 days before your qualifying date will result in a refusal and your fee will not be returned.

---

**You can apply for British citizenship 12 months after the date your ILR was granted, not the date your ILR application was approved or your BRP arrived. The 28-day rule allows you to submit up to 28 days early. You must also meet absence limits: no more than 450 days outside the UK in the 5 years before applying, and no more than 90 days in the final 12 months before your application date. Missing either limit means you must wait longer.**

## Introduction

Timing your citizenship application correctly saves you money and avoids a refusal. Most applicants on a Skilled Worker, Global Talent, or family visa need to wait 12 months after their ILR is granted. Read [what ILR is and how it differs from citizenship](/articles/ilr-vs-british-citizenship) before applying if you are unsure of the difference. This guide explains every timing rule, absence check, and route variation so you know your exact eligibility date.

---

## How to Find Your ILR Grant Date

Your ILR grant date is the date the Home Office approved your settlement. It is **not** the date your BRP card arrived or your visa expiry date.

**If you have an eVisa:** Log in to your UKVI account. Your status page shows the date your leave was granted.

**If you had a BRP:** The grant date is shown on your ILR decision letter from the Home Office. It is also sometimes shown on the BRP itself, but the decision letter is the definitive source.

**Do not use the BRP expiry date.** A BRP card can show a date of 31 December 2024 even if your ILR has no expiry — ILR is permanent leave and does not expire.

---

## The Standard 12-Month Rule

After your ILR is granted, you must wait 12 full calendar months before your citizenship application date. This applies to most routes, including:

- Skilled Worker (5-year route)
- Global Talent
- Scale-up visa
- Innovator Founder
- Long residence (10-year route)
- Family visas (non-spouse routes)

**Example:** ILR granted on 15 March 2025. Your qualifying date is 15 March 2026. You can submit your application from 15 February 2026 (28 days before).

---

## The Spouse and Civil Partner Route

If you are the spouse or civil partner of a British citizen, the rules differ slightly. You need **3 years of continuous residence in the UK** combined with holding ILR. The spouse route does not require an additional 12-month post-ILR wait — 3 years' total residence and ILR at the point of application is sufficient.

You do not need to wait an additional 12 months after ILR if your total UK residence already meets the 3-year threshold at the time you receive ILR. Check [British citizenship by marriage requirements](/articles/british-citizenship-requirements-2026) for the full spouse-route rules.

---

## The 28-Day Early Application Window

You can submit your application **up to 28 days before your qualifying date**. This rule exists so people can plan around appointment availability and processing times.

**Rules for the 28-day window:**
- Count 28 calendar days back from your qualifying date
- Day 1 of the window is 28 days before your qualifying date
- Submitting on day 29 or earlier = refusal, fee lost
- The Home Office checks this automatically

This is particularly useful because UKVCAS biometric appointment slots can take 1–3 weeks to secure. Submit the online form first, then book biometrics immediately.

---

## Absence Rules Before Applying

Meeting the 12-month ILR wait is only one part of the test. You must also meet two absence limits, measured from your **application date** (not your ILR date).

### The 5-Year Absence Limit

In the 5 years immediately before your application date, you must not have been outside the UK for more than **450 days in total**. This applies across all routes.

If you have been abroad for work, family emergencies, or long holidays, count every day carefully. Even a single day over the limit means you must wait until your absence count drops below 450 days.

### The 12-Month Absence Limit

In the 12 months immediately before your application date, you must not have been absent for more than **90 days**.

This is the stricter limit and catches many applicants off guard. A long holiday or extended work trip abroad in the year before you apply can push your eligibility date back significantly.

**Example:** You planned to apply on 1 July 2026. But between July 2025 and June 2026, you were abroad for 95 days. You must wait until a later date when your rolling 12-month absence count falls below 90 days.

---

## What Happens If You Apply Too Early

If you submit your application more than 28 days before your qualifying date:

- The Home Office will refuse your application
- Your **fee will not be refunded** (£1,839)
- You can reapply once your qualifying date arrives
- There is no penalty beyond the financial loss — you can apply again immediately

This is one of the most common and costly mistakes. Use the [Citizenship Planner](/citizenship-planner) to confirm your exact submission window before you pay.

---

## What Happens If You Have Too Many Absences

If your absences exceed either limit, you cannot simply wait for the 12-month ILR period to pass. You must wait until:

- Your rolling 5-year absence count drops below 450 days, AND
- Your rolling 12-month absence count drops below 90 days

As days pass, old trips fall out of the 5-year and 12-month windows. Keep a spreadsheet of all your departure and return dates to track this precisely.

---

## When You Cannot Apply Yet

You cannot submit a citizenship application if:

- Your ILR application is still pending — you must have a decision
- You have an outstanding appeal or administrative review on your immigration status
- You are currently outside the UK and your ILR has lapsed due to extended absence
- You have serious criminal convictions — see [good character requirements](/articles/good-character-requirement-ilr-citizenship) for what counts

---

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Using the BRP expiry date as the ILR grant date** — BRP cards often show 31 December 2024 as a placeholder. This is not your ILR grant date. Your actual grant date is on your decision letter.

❌ **Applying on exactly the qualifying date without the 28-day window in mind** — Many applicants assume the earliest they can apply is the 12-month anniversary itself. In fact, you can apply 28 days before that date.

❌ **Ignoring the 90-day absence check** — Applicants focus on the 5-year limit and overlook the 12-month limit. Exceeding 90 days abroad in the year before applying pushes back your eligibility.

❌ **Applying while ILR is still pending** — Some applicants submit a citizenship application while their ILR decision is outstanding. The Home Office will refuse it. Wait for your ILR decision first.

❌ **Not checking for criminal records or cautions** — A spent caution from several years ago can still affect good character assessments. Get advice before applying if you have any record.

---

## Expert Tips

1. **Calculate both absence limits independently.** Use a spreadsheet or the [Absence Calculator](/absence-calculator) to track your 5-year total and your rolling 12-month count separately. The 12-month limit is the one most people miss.

2. **Set your application window in your calendar.** Mark the 28-day opening date and the qualifying date. Plan to submit in the first week of your window so you have time to fix any issues before the deadline.

3. **Book your UKVCAS biometric appointment immediately after submitting.** Do not wait. Slots in busy areas fill up within hours. A delayed biometrics appointment does not delay the Home Office clock — but it does delay your certificate of naturalisation.

4. **Request your decision letter if you have lost it.** If you cannot find your ILR decision letter and your eVisa does not clearly show a grant date, contact UKVI to confirm the exact date in writing before you apply.

5. **Check the [ILR 28-day rule](/articles/ilr-28-day-rule-uk) article separately.** That rule governs when you can apply for ILR. The citizenship 28-day window is a different but similarly named rule — do not confuse the two.

## FAQs

### When can I apply for British citizenship after ILR?

You can apply 12 months after the date your ILR was granted. You can also submit your application up to 28 days before that 12-month anniversary. Most applicants on work and family routes follow this rule.

### Can I apply for citizenship while waiting for ILR?

No. You must have a granted ILR decision before you can apply for citizenship. A pending ILR application does not count. Wait for the Home Office to make a decision on your ILR before submitting your citizenship application.

### How many days can I be outside the UK before applying for citizenship?

You must not have been outside the UK for more than 450 days in the 5 years before your application date, and no more than 90 days in the 12 months immediately before your application date. Both limits must be met.

### Does the 28-day rule apply to citizenship as well as ILR?

Yes, but these are separate rules. For ILR, the 28-day rule means you can apply up to 28 days before your qualifying period ends. For citizenship, the 28-day rule means you can apply up to 28 days before your 12-month post-ILR date. They work the same way but apply at different stages.

### What is the citizenship eligibility date for a spouse of a British citizen?

If you are the spouse or civil partner of a British citizen, you need 3 years of continuous residence in the UK and must hold ILR at the point of application. No additional 12-month post-ILR wait — you need 3 years total residence and ILR at the point of application.

### What happens if I apply for citizenship too early?

If you apply more than 28 days before your qualifying date, the Home Office will refuse your application. Your fee (£1,839 in 2026) will not be refunded. You can reapply once your qualifying date arrives.

### Can I speed up the citizenship application?

There is no standard priority service for British citizenship applications. Processing times are typically up to 6 months. You cannot pay to accelerate the decision.
