**Officially, you find out on the day you take the test — but in practice, many candidates do not see their result on screen or get told verbally, and instead wait for an email.** That email contains your Unique Reference Number (URN), which is your actual proof of passing — not a printed certificate or "pass notification letter." Real test-takers report the email arriving anywhere from a few minutes after the test to several hours later, and occasionally not until the following morning. Find out more about [whether your result ever expires](/articles/does-the-life-in-the-uk-test-certificate-expire) — it does not.

---

Some test centres do tell candidates their result verbally as they collect their belongings, but this is not consistent — it depends on the centre and the staff on duty. **Do not assume you'll be told on the spot.** Check your email (including spam) once you've left, and if nothing has arrived after several hours, you can also check your result by logging into your PSI Life in the UK (LitUK) account.

## What Actually Happens the Moment You Finish

The moment you click Submit on your final answer, the test software scores your responses automatically. There is no manual marking and no suspense built into the scoring itself — the computer knows your result instantly.

The two outcomes are:

- **PASS** — you answered 18 or more of the 24 questions correctly (75% or above)
- **FAIL** — you answered 17 or fewer correctly

What happens *next*, though, varies more than many guides suggest. The Home Office's own caseworker guidance states that *"people are told if they have passed or failed on the day they take the test."* In practice, real test-takers report a genuine mix: some are told verbally by staff as they collect their belongings, while many others get no on-the-spot confirmation at all and simply wait for an email.

---

## What You Receive If You Pass

You do not receive a certificate or a printed letter handed to you at the centre. Since 17 December 2019, the test provider (PSI) has stopped issuing paper pass notification letters. Instead, your proof of passing is your **Unique Reference Number (URN)** — sent to you by email on the day you sit the test, in the format `HOM/010114/123456/123456789`, and viewable in your online PSI Life in the UK (LitUK) account.

That email is the single most important record from your test day. Real test-takers report it landing anywhere from a few minutes after finishing to several hours later — and occasionally not until the following morning. Save it, screenshot it, forward it to a second account, and write the URN down somewhere durable. You will need this number — not a piece of paper — when you apply for ILR or British citizenship. The Home Office uses your URN to verify your result; there is nothing physical to keep.

Your result is also stored permanently on the Home Office system, linked to your URN. Caseworkers look the number up directly — you do not post or upload any document.

## What You Receive If You Fail

If your result is a fail, you also receive an email confirming this — there is simply no URN to use afterwards. You are not given a breakdown of which questions you got wrong, only your overall outcome.

This is not the end of the road. There is no limit on how many times you can retake the test. Each retake costs £50. See the full guide on [how many times you can fail the Life in the UK test](/articles/how-many-times-can-you-fail-life-in-the-uk-test) — the answer is as many times as you need.

You can start a new booking the same day, but leave at least 7 days between your test *dates* — test takers consistently report being turned away at the centre, without a refund, for sitting again sooner than that. Use that time to identify your weak areas and practise specifically on those chapters before you rebook. See [how to approach a second attempt](/articles/life-in-the-uk-test-second-attempt).

## Can You Check Your Results Online?

Yes — via your PSI Life in the UK (LitUK) online account, where your result and URN are recorded. This account, alongside the result email, is the official replacement for the old paper letter system that ended in December 2019.

If you cannot find your result email or have forgotten your URN, check your PSI/LitUK account first. If you still cannot locate your details, contact the official helpline (support@lituk.psionline.com / 0800 015 4245) — they can verify your identity and help retrieve your details, though this can take time. See the full guide on [what to do if you've lost your result](/articles/lost-life-in-the-uk-test-certificate) for the exact steps.

## How Long Is Your Result Valid?

Your Life in the UK test pass is valid forever. There is no expiry date on your result or your URN.

You could pass the test in 2026 and use the same result for a citizenship application in 2035. The Home Office accepts passes regardless of when the test was taken.

This also means there is no rush to apply for ILR or citizenship immediately after passing. Take your time. Gather all your other documents. Apply when you are genuinely ready. Your [result never expires](/articles/does-the-life-in-the-uk-test-certificate-expire) — it remains valid for every future application.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Assuming you'll be told your result on the spot, then not checking your email afterwards**
Some test centres tell candidates verbally; many do not. If you walk out assuming "no news at the desk means I'll never hear," you risk missing the email that contains your URN — your only proof. Check your inbox (and spam folder) over the following hours, and don't be alarmed if it takes until the next morning.

❌ **Waiting for a certificate or letter that will never arrive**
No certificate or paper "pass notification letter" has been issued since 17 December 2019. If you are waiting at home for something to be posted to you, you will be waiting indefinitely. What you need is already in your inbox: the result email containing your URN.

❌ **Losing or deleting the result email**
This email is your only record — there is no certificate to fall back on. Forward it to a second account, screenshot it, and write the URN down somewhere durable the day you receive it.

❌ **Assuming a fail means you must wait before you can rebook**
You can start a new booking the moment you decide to. The 7-day guidance is about the gap between your test *dates*, not when you can register your intention to retake — though leaving less than 7 days between sittings risks being turned away at the centre with no refund.

## Expert Tips

1. **Forward your result email to a second account and screenshot it the moment it arrives.** There is no certificate to fall back on — this email and your URN are the entire record. A second copy elsewhere is cheap insurance against losing access to one inbox.

2. **Write your URN down somewhere secure, separate from the email.** It's the single most important detail — the number that links your result to the Home Office database. Having it noted separately means you can quote it accurately under pressure.

3. **Don't panic if nothing arrives within the hour.** Real test-takers report delays of several hours, and occasionally overnight. Check spam first, then log into your PSI Life in the UK (LitUK) account, where your result is also recorded.

4. **Double-check the URN against your immigration documents the same day.** The format is long (`HOM/010114/123456/123456789`) and easy to mistype. Copy it directly from the email rather than retyping from memory when you come to use it.

5. **If you fail, do not rebook before you are scoring 90%+ in practice.** The pass mark is 75%, but practising to that level puts you right on the borderline. Score consistently above 90% in [mock exams](/mock-test) before rebooking.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When do you get the results for the Life in the UK test?

Officially, on the day you take it — the Home Office's own guidance confirms this. In practice, it varies: some candidates are told verbally as they leave, but many others simply receive an email later that day (occasionally the next morning) containing their Unique Reference Number (URN), which is their actual proof of the result.

### Do Life in the UK test results come by post?

No — and nothing physical is sent at all. Since 17 December 2019, results have been delivered by email (containing your URN) and through your online PSI Life in the UK (LitUK) account. The old paper "pass notification letter" system no longer exists.

### Can I check my Life in the UK test results online?

Yes. Your result is recorded in your PSI Life in the UK (LitUK) online account, and you also receive a confirmation email containing your URN if you pass. This replaced the old in-person paper letter system in December 2019.

### What happens if I lose my result email or forget my URN?

Your result is stored permanently on the Home Office system, so the result itself is not lost. Check your PSI/LitUK online account first — your URN is shown there. If you still can't find it, contact the official helpline (support@lituk.psionline.com / 0800 015 4245) to verify your identity and retrieve your details. See our guide on [what to do if you've lost your result](/articles/lost-life-in-the-uk-test-certificate).

### How long after passing do I need to apply for ILR?

There is no deadline for using your result. It is valid for life and never expires. You apply for ILR when you meet all the other requirements — typically 5 years' continuous residence on a qualifying visa. Your URN will still be valid whenever you are ready. Use our [free practice questions](/practice) to prepare if you have not yet sat the test.

### What does the Life in the UK test result email contain?

It confirms whether you passed or failed and, if you passed, includes your Unique Reference Number (URN) in the format `HOM/010114/123456/123456789`. This number is what you enter on your ILR or citizenship application — caseworkers use it to verify your result directly against the Home Office system.
