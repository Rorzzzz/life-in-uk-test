## Introduction

Passing the Life in the UK test is a requirement for most people applying for ILR or British citizenship. The test is the same at every approved centre in the UK. This guide explains how to find and book a Manchester test centre, what to bring, and what to expect on the day. For a full overview of the test format, see [what to expect on the day](/articles/life-in-the-uk-test-what-to-expect-on-the-day). To understand what ID you need, see [what ID do you need for the Life in the UK test](/articles/what-id-do-you-need-for-the-life-in-the-uk-test).

## Finding a Manchester Test Centre

Test centre addresses are managed by PSI Services, the company that runs the Life in the UK test on behalf of the Home Office. Addresses can change. Do not rely on lists from third-party websites — they may show old or closed locations.

**The correct way to find the current Manchester address:**

1. Go to **gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test**
2. Click "Book your test"
3. Enter a Manchester postcode (e.g. M1 1AE, M2 4WU, or your own postcode)
4. The system shows available centres nearby with current addresses and available dates

To find the current centre address(es) serving Greater Manchester, go to gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test and enter a Manchester postcode. Centre details change — always use the live booking system.

## How to Book Your Manchester Test

Booking takes around 10 minutes if you have your ID details ready.

**What you need before booking:**

- Your ID document (passport or Biometric Residence Permit — you will use this same document on test day)
- Your home address
- An email address for confirmation
- A debit or credit card to pay the £50 fee

**Steps:**

1. Go to gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test
2. Click "Book your test"
3. Select English as your test language (or Welsh if preferred)
4. Enter a Manchester postcode to find nearby centres
5. Choose your preferred centre, date, and time
6. Enter your personal and ID details
7. Pay the £50 fee online
8. Receive your confirmation email — save it

Your confirmation email includes the centre address, your appointment time, and your booking reference. Print it or save it on your phone.

## How Far in Advance Should You Book?

Slots in Manchester can fill up quickly, especially for popular daytime times. Typical waiting time for a slot is **1–3 weeks — check the GOV.UK booking system for current slots.

If you need to sit the test on a specific date (for example, because your ILR application has a deadline), book as early as possible. The booking system opens slots several weeks ahead.

For guidance on when to book relative to your ILR or citizenship application, see [when should I book the Life in the UK test](/articles/when-should-i-book-the-life-in-the-uk-test) and [ILR processing time 2026](/articles/ilr-processing-time-2026).

## Can I Book a Different Centre?

Yes. You are not required to sit the test at a centre near your home. You can book any approved centre anywhere in England, Scotland, Wales, or Northern Ireland.

If Manchester slots are full or inconvenient, consider:
- Salford
- Bolton
- Stockport
- Bury
- Rochdale
- Wigan

Enter postcodes from those towns in the booking system to find their nearest centres and availability. The test and fee are identical at every approved centre.

## Evening and Weekend Slots

Evening and weekend appointments are available at some Manchester centres at £75. Slots within 24 hours cost £80. These are useful if you cannot attend during weekday daytime hours.

The test content, format, and duration are the same for evening and weekend sittings. Only the fee differs.

## What to Bring to Your Manchester Test

**Bring:** The exact ID document you used when registering for the test. This must be the same document — if you registered with your passport, bring your passport. If you registered with your BRP, bring your BRP.

**Leave behind:** Everything else. You cannot bring phones, bags, notes, food, or drinks into the test room. The centre provides a locker or reception area for personal items.

**Also bring:** Your booking confirmation (printed or on your phone) — though the centre can look you up by name and date of birth if needed.

---

## What Happens on the Day

Arrive at least 10–15 minutes before your appointment time. Late arrivals may not be admitted and will lose their fee.

At the centre:

1. **Sign in** at reception and show your ID
2. **Leave all personal items** in lockers or at reception
3. **Wait** to be called into the test room
4. **Sit your test** — 24 questions, 45-minute time limit, computer-based
5. **Submit your answers** when ready — you can review questions before submitting
6. **Results appear on screen** immediately after you submit

---

## If You Pass

No certificate or letter is printed at the centre — that process ended in December 2019. Instead, you receive an email containing your **Unique Reference Number (URN)**, usually on the day (sometimes with a delay of a few hours), and your result is also stored in your online PSI Life in the UK (LitUK) account. Save that email and write the URN down — you need it for your ILR and citizenship applications. If you lose it, see [what to do if you've lost your result](/articles/lost-life-in-the-uk-test-certificate).

---

## If You Fail

The screen shows a fail notification immediately. You can rebook after **7 days** — there is no waiting period beyond this and no limit on the number of attempts. Each attempt costs £50.

Many people pass on a second or third attempt after focused revision. ## Common Mistakes

❌ **Using an old address from a third-party website** — Test centre addresses change. Always verify the current address by booking through gov.uk or checking your confirmation email.

❌ **Bringing the wrong ID document** — If you registered with your passport, you must bring your passport on test day. Bringing a different document may result in being refused entry and losing your fee.

❌ **Arriving late** — Manchester centres are strict about arrival times. The test starts on a schedule. Arrive at least 10 minutes early to sign in, store your belongings, and settle.

❌ **Confusing the booking confirmation address with a permanent centre address** — Always use the address in your confirmation email. Do not navigate to an address you remember from a previous booking.

❌ **Not keeping your pass reference number safely** — The reference number from your pass certificate is required for ILR and citizenship applications. Store it in a secure location immediately.

## FAQs

### Where is the Life in the UK test centre in Manchester?

The exact address changes and is managed by PSI Services. Find the current Manchester address by entering a postcode (such as M1 1AE) into the booking tool at gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test. Your confirmation email also shows the exact address.

### How much does the Life in the UK test cost in Manchester?

The standard fee for a weekday daytime test is £50. Evening and weekend slots cost £75. Slots within the next 24 hours cost £80. The fee is paid when you book online and is not refundable if you miss your appointment.

### How long does the Life in the UK test take in Manchester?

The test allows 45 minutes for 24 questions. Most people finish in 20–30 minutes. Results are shown on screen immediately after you submit. Plan for around 1 hour at the centre including sign-in and waiting time.

### Can I book the Manchester test centre if I live outside Greater Manchester?

Yes. You can book any approved test centre in the UK regardless of where you live. If you find it more convenient to sit the test in Manchester, you can book there even if you live in a different area.

### What happens if I fail the Life in the UK test in Manchester?

You can rebook after 7 days. There is no limit on the number of attempts. Each attempt costs £50. Use the time between attempts to practise with [chapter-by-chapter questions](/practice) and [full mock tests](/mock-test).

### Do I need to bring anything other than my ID to the Manchester test?

Bring your ID document (the same one used when registering) and your booking confirmation. Everything else — phone, bags, notes — must be left outside the test room.
