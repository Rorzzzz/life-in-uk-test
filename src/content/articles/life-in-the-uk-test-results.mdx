**Officially, you find out on the day you take the test — but in practice, many candidates do not see their result on screen or get told verbally, and instead wait for an email.** That email contains your Unique Reference Number (URN), which is your actual proof of passing — not a printed certificate. Real test-takers report the email arriving anywhere from a few minutes after the test to several hours later, and occasionally not until the following morning. There is no longer a physical "pass notification letter" handed to you at the centre — that process stopped in December 2019.

Some test centres do tell candidates their result verbally as they collect their belongings, but this is not consistent — it depends on the centre and the staff on duty. **Do not assume you'll be told on the spot.** Check your email (including spam) once you've left, and if nothing has arrived after several hours, you can also check your result by logging into your PSI Life in the UK (LitUK) account.

## What Happens the Moment You Finish

When you submit your last answer, the test is marked automatically — there's no manual checking and no suspense period built into the scoring itself. The two outcomes are:

- **PASS** — you answered 18 or more of the 24 questions correctly (75% or above)
- **FAIL** — you answered 17 or fewer correctly

What happens next, though, varies more than most guides admit. Officially, the Home Office's own caseworker guidance states that *"people are told if they have passed or failed on the day they take the test."* In practice, test-takers report a real mix: some are told verbally by staff as they collect their belongings, while many others get no on-the-spot confirmation at all and simply wait for an email.

### If You Pass

You do not receive a certificate. Since 17 December 2019, the test provider (PSI) has not issued paper pass notification letters. Instead, your proof of passing is your **Unique Reference Number (URN)** — sent to you by email on the day you take the test, and viewable in your online PSI Life in the UK (LitUK) account.

That email is the single most important document from your test day. Real test-takers report it landing anywhere from a few minutes after finishing to several hours later — and occasionally not until the following morning. Save it, screenshot it, and store the URN somewhere durable (your phone, your email, and a note with your other immigration documents). You will need this number — not a piece of paper — when you apply for ILR or British citizenship.

### If You Fail

You also receive an email confirming your result — there is simply no URN to use afterwards. You can start a new booking the same day, but leave at least 7 days between your test *dates* — test takers consistently report being turned away at the centre, without a refund, for sitting again sooner than that. Most people also find 2–4 weeks of additional study makes a significant difference to their result.

See the full guide on [what happens if you fail](/articles/what-happens-if-you-fail-the-life-in-the-uk-test) and [how to rebook](/articles/failed-life-in-the-uk-test-how-to-rebook).

## Your URN — What to Do With It

Your Unique Reference Number is the official proof that you passed the Life in the UK test. It's needed for:

- **Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) applications** — entered on your SET(O) or equivalent form
- **British citizenship (naturalisation) applications** — entered on your AN form
- **Any other application that requires proof of the Life in the UK test**

You do not need to apply immediately after passing. Because [your result never expires](/articles/does-the-life-in-the-uk-test-certificate-expire), you can pass the test months or years before you apply — your URN remains valid for whenever you are ready to use it. Caseworkers verify your result against the Home Office system using this number, so the email containing it (or a note of the number itself) is what you need to keep — not a physical certificate, which simply isn't issued anymore.

## What If You Lose Your Result Email or Forget Your URN

Your result is held permanently on the Home Office system — losing the email does not mean losing your pass. If you cannot find the email or remember your URN, you can check your PSI Life in the UK (LitUK) online account, or contact the official helpline (support@lituk.psionline.com / 0800 015 4245) to retrieve your details. This involves identity verification and can take time, so it's far simpler to save the email and note the number down the day you receive it.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Assuming you'll be told your result on the spot, then leaving without checking your email later**
Some test centres tell candidates verbally; many do not. If you walk out assuming "no news at the desk means I'll never hear," you risk missing the email that contains your URN — the only proof you'll get. Check your inbox (and spam folder) over the following hours, and don't panic if it takes until the next morning.

❌ **Deleting or losing the result email**
There is no certificate to fall back on if you lose this email — the URN inside it is your entire record. Forward it to a second account, screenshot it, and write the number down somewhere durable the moment it arrives.

❌ **Assuming a "pass certificate" will be posted to you**
Plenty of older guides — and some AI-generated summaries — describe a printed certificate or "pass notification letter" arriving by post or being handed over at the centre. That process ended in December 2019. Nothing physical is sent. If you're waiting for a letter, you'll be waiting indefinitely — what you actually need is already in your inbox.

❌ **Assuming you can request which questions you got wrong after failing**
If you fail, you are told your overall score but not which specific questions you answered incorrectly. The test centre does not provide a question-by-question breakdown.
Use [practice questions](/practice) to identify your weak areas — not the test result itself, which gives you a score but no diagnostic information.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do you find out immediately if you pass the Life in the UK test?

Officially, yes — the Home Office's own guidance says you're told on the day. In practice, it's inconsistent: some test centres tell you verbally as you leave, but many candidates report no on-the-spot confirmation and instead wait for the result email, which can take anywhere from minutes to several hours (occasionally until the next morning).

### Do you get the Life in the UK test results by email?

Yes — this is now the main way most people actually find out. You receive an email containing your Unique Reference Number (URN) on the day you sit the test, whether you passed or failed. This replaced the old paper "pass notification letter" system in December 2019.

### Do you get a printed certificate if you pass?

No. Despite what many guides still say, no certificate or paper letter has been issued since December 2019. Your proof of passing is your URN — delivered by email and viewable in your online PSI/LitUK account. Caseworkers verify your result against the Home Office system using this number, not a physical document.

### How long is a Life in the UK test pass valid for?

Permanently. Your result never expires and can be used for any future ILR or citizenship application regardless of how much time has passed since you sat the test. See the full [validity guide](/articles/does-the-life-in-the-uk-test-certificate-expire).

### Can I take the test again if I fail?

Yes — there is no limit on retakes. You can start a new booking immediately, but leave at least 7 days between your test dates: test takers consistently report being turned away (with no refund) for arriving sooner than that. Each attempt costs £50, and most people benefit from 2–4 additional weeks of practice before reattempting. See [how to approach a second attempt](/articles/life-in-the-uk-test-second-attempt).

### Do both spouses get separate certificates?

Yes. Each person must sit the test individually and receives their own certificate. A certificate cannot be shared or used for a partner's application. See the guide on [whether both spouses need to take the test](/articles/do-both-spouses-need-to-take-the-life-in-the-uk-test).

## Expert Tips

**1. Forward and save your result email the moment it arrives.**
There is no certificate to fall back on. Forward the email to a second account, take a screenshot, and write the URN down somewhere durable — your phone notes, a printed copy with your passport. This is the only record you'll have.

**2. You do not need to apply immediately after passing.**
Many people pass the test before they are eligible to apply for ILR or citizenship — for example, before they have accumulated the required years of residence. That is fine. Your result and URN are valid indefinitely.

**3. If several hours pass with no email, don't panic — check spam, then your online account.**
Real test-takers report delays of several hours and occasionally overnight. Before assuming something has gone wrong, check your spam folder and log into your PSI Life in the UK (LitUK) account, where your result is also recorded.
