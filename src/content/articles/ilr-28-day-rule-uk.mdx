You can apply for ILR up to 28 days before your 5-year qualifying date. This early application window counts as meeting the qualifying period. If you apply more than 28 days before your qualifying date, your application will be rejected.

## What Is the ILR Qualifying Date?

Your qualifying date is the date you become eligible to apply for ILR. For most routes, this is exactly 5 years after your first qualifying leave was granted.

**Example:**
Your Skilled Worker visa was granted on 15 June 2021. Your qualifying date is 15 June 2026. You can apply from 18 May 2026 — 28 days before 15 June.

Your qualifying date is not the same as your visa expiry date. Your visa may expire in August 2026, but your qualifying date could be June 2026. Check both dates separately.

---

## How the 28-Day Rule Works

The 28-day window opens exactly 28 days before your qualifying date.

When you apply within this window:

1. The Home Office treats your application as if submitted on your qualifying date
2. The 28-day period counts toward your 5-year qualifying period
3. Your ILR is not backdated — but the early application is accepted as valid

This rule exists so applicants can plan ahead. Without it, you would need to apply on a single exact date to avoid any gap in leave.

---

## What Happens If You Apply Too Early?

If you apply more than 28 days before your qualifying date, the Home Office will reject or refuse your application.

The outcome depends on how the application is handled:

- If returned before processing, fees may be refunded minus an administration charge
- If refused after consideration, the £3,226 non-refundable fee per person is likely lost

Check your qualifying date carefully before applying. Calculate it from the date shown on your original visa grant letter — not from your passport stamp or entry date.

## Working Out Your Qualifying Date

Your qualifying date depends on your route.

**Skilled Worker / Health and Care Worker:**
5 years from the date your first visa on this route was granted.

**Family (Spouse / Civil Partner):**
Usually 5 years from the date of your first entry on a family visa. Check your specific grant letter.

**Global Talent:**
Qualifying period is 3 years for all Global Talent visa holders, regardless of endorsing body.

**Long Residence:**
10 years of continuous lawful residence — the qualifying date is 10 years from the start of your continuous lawful stay.

If you have had gaps in qualifying leave, your qualifying date may be later than expected. Use the [ILR eligibility calculator](/ilr-eligibility-calculator) to confirm your date.

## Does Absence Affect Your Qualifying Date?

For most ILR routes, you must not have spent more than 180 days outside the UK in any 12-month rolling period during the qualifying period.

Excessive absences do not reset your qualifying date. But they can mean you do not meet the qualifying criteria even if 5 years have passed on a qualifying visa.

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Applying before the 28-day window opens**
The most expensive mistake in this process. Submitting your application 30, 40, or 60 days before your qualifying date will result in rejection. The £3,226 fee per person is at risk. Calculate 28 days back from your exact qualifying date before submitting.

❌ **Confusing visa expiry with qualifying date**
Your visa may expire months after your qualifying date — or before it. They are different things. Your qualifying date determines ILR eligibility. Your visa expiry determines when your current leave ends. Check both.

❌ **Not checking absences before applying**
Excessive absences during the qualifying period can make you ineligible even after 5 years. Run all travel dates through the [absence calculator](/absence-calculator) before you apply.

❌ **Waiting until the last moment**
If your visa expires in 2 months and your qualifying date is next week, apply now — inside the 28-day window. Processing takes time. If your visa expires before a decision is made, Section 3C leave will extend your status while the application is pending.

❌ **Not having the right qualifying leave**
Not all visas count toward ILR. Time on a Visitor visa, Short-term Student visa, or Graduate visa does not count. Only qualifying leave counts. Check the [ILR documents guide](/ilr-documents-required-2026) to confirm your visa route qualifies.

## Expert Tips

**1. Set a calendar alert for your qualifying date minus 28 days.** The day that alert fires, your window is open. There is no benefit to waiting — start your application immediately.

**2. Check your qualifying date on your original visa grant letter, not your passport.** Passport entry stamps are not always accurate for this calculation. The grant letter date is what the Home Office uses.

**3. If you have had multiple visas on the same route, your qualifying date is 5 years from the first grant.** You do not reset the clock each time you renew a visa.

**4. Gather your documents before the window opens.** The [ILR checklist](/ilr-checklist) lists everything you need. Have it ready to submit as soon as the window opens.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the ILR 28-day rule?

The ILR 28-day rule allows you to submit your application up to 28 days before your qualifying date. The 28-day period counts toward the 5-year qualifying period. Applying inside this window is treated the same as applying on your exact qualifying date.

### What happens if I apply for ILR more than 28 days early?

Your application will be rejected. The Home Office will not accept an ILR application submitted more than 28 days before your qualifying date. The £3,226 application fee per person is at risk if the application is refused rather than returned.

### How do I calculate my ILR qualifying date?

Your qualifying date is 5 years from the date your first qualifying visa was granted. For Skilled Worker visa holders, this is the date on the original visa grant letter. Use the [ILR eligibility calculator](/ilr-eligibility-calculator) to confirm your exact date.

### Can I apply for ILR early if my visa is about to expire?

Yes — if your qualifying date falls within your current visa period and you are within 28 days of that date. Submit your application before your visa expires. If your visa expires while your application is pending, Section 3C leave extends your right to stay.

### Does absence affect when I can apply for ILR?

Excessive absence during the qualifying period can make you ineligible even after 5 years on a qualifying visa. Check your travel history with the [absence calculator](/absence-calculator) before submitting your application.
